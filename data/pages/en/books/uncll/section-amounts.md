<a id="section-amounts"></a>11.5. <a id="c11s5"></a>Amount abstractions
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

The following cmavo is discussed in this section:

<table class="cmavo-list"><colgroup></colgroup><tbody><tr class="cmavo-entry"><td class="cmavo"><p class="cmavo">ni</p></td><td class="selmaho"><p class="selmaho">NU</p></td><td class="description"><p class="description">amount abstraction</p></td></tr></tbody></table>

Amount abstractions are far more limited than event or property abstractions. They really make sense only if the selbri of the abstracted bridi is subject to measurement of some sort. Thus we can speak of:

<div class="interlinear-gloss-example example">
<a id="example-random-id-QW2C"></a>

**Example 11.33. <a id="c11e5d1"></a>** 

<table class="interlinear-gloss"><colgroup></colgroup><tbody><tr class="jbo"><td>le</td><td>ni</td><td>le</td><td>pixra</td><td>cu</td><td>blanu</td><td>[kei]</td></tr><tr class="gloss"><td>the</td><td>amount-of</td><td>(the</td><td>picture</td><td>&nbsp;</td><td>being-blue</td><td>)</td></tr></tbody></table>

<table class="interlinear-gloss"><tbody><tr class="para"><td colspan="12321"><p class="natlang">the amount of blueness in the picture</p></td></tr></tbody></table>

</div>  

because “blueness” could be measured with a colorimeter or a similar device. However,

<div class="interlinear-gloss-example example">
<a id="example-random-id-FyL4"></a>

**Example 11.34. <a id="c11e5d2"></a>** 

<table class="interlinear-gloss"><colgroup></colgroup><tbody><tr class="jbo"><td>le</td><td>ni</td><td>la</td><td>.djein.</td><td>cu</td><td>mamta</td><td>[kei]</td></tr><tr class="gloss"><td>the</td><td>amount-of</td><td>(that-named</td><td>Jane</td><td>&nbsp;</td><td>being-a-mother</td><td>)</td></tr></tbody></table>

<table class="interlinear-gloss"><tbody><tr class="para"><td colspan="12321"><p class="natlang">the amount of Jane's mother-ness (?)</p></td></tr><tr class="para"><td colspan="12321"><p class="natlang">the amount of mother-ness in Jane (?)</p></td></tr></tbody></table>

</div>  

makes very little sense in either Lojban or English. We simply do not have any sort of measurement scale for being a mother.

Semantically, a sumti with _<a id="id-1.12.7.9.1.1" class="indexterm"></a>le ni_ is a number; however, it cannot be treated grammatically as a quantifier in Lojban unless prefixed by the mathematical cmavo _<a id="id-1.12.7.9.2.1" class="indexterm"></a>[_mo'e_](../go01#valsi-mohe)_ :

<div class="interlinear-gloss-example example">
<a id="example-random-id-SaTi"></a>

**Example 11.35. <a id="c11e5d3"></a>** 

<table class="interlinear-gloss"><colgroup></colgroup><tbody><tr class="jbo"><td>li</td><td>pa</td><td>vu'u</td><td>mo'e</td><td>le</td><td>ni</td><td>&nbsp;</td></tr><tr class="gloss"><td>the-number</td><td>1</td><td>minus</td><td>the-operand</td><td>the</td><td>amount-of</td><td>(</td></tr></tbody></table>

<table class="interlinear-gloss"><colgroup></colgroup><tbody><tr class="jbo"><td>le</td><td>pixra</td><td>cu</td><td>blanu</td><td>[kei]</td></tr><tr class="gloss"><td>the</td><td>picture</td><td>&nbsp;</td><td>being-blue</td><td>)</td></tr></tbody></table>

<table class="interlinear-gloss"><tbody><tr class="para"><td colspan="12321"><p class="natlang"><span class="mathphrase">1 - B</span> , where <span class="mathphrase">B</span> = blueness of the picture</p></td></tr></tbody></table>

</div>  

Mathematical Lojban is beyond the scope of this chapter, and is explained more fully in [Chapter 18](../chapter-mekso).

Whenever we talk of measurement of an amount, there is some sort of scale, and so the place structure of _<a id="id-1.12.7.12.1.1" class="indexterm"></a>[_ni_](../go01#valsi-ni)_ abstraction selbri is:

> _<a id="id-1.12.7.13.1.1.1" class="indexterm"></a>[_ni_](../go01#valsi-ni)_ x1 is the amount of (the bridi) on scale x2

Note: the best way to express the x2 places of abstract sumti is to use something like _<a id="id-1.12.7.14.2.1" class="indexterm"></a>le ni ... kei be_. See [Example 11.59](../section-minor-abstractions#example-random-id-zvfX) for the use of this construction.
