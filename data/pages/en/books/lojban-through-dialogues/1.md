## Lesson 1. Hello and Goodbye

<speaker sprite="lif2">Lifri has just spotted Mentu at a Lojban meetup. It's his first time here. Let's listen.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| coi | Hello. |
| coi | Hello! |
| mi'e la lifri | I'm Lifri. |
| mi'e la mentu | I'm Mentu. |
| coi la mentu | Hello, Mentu. |
| coi la lifri | Hello, Lifri. |
| co'o | Goodbye. |
| co'o la lifri | Goodbye, Lifri. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| coi | hello (greeting) |
| co'o | goodbye (parting) |
| mi'e | I am … / my name is … |
| la | name-tag — turns the next word into a name |
| do | you |

> **Grammar note:** A single Lojban word can be a complete sentence. **coi** on its own means "I greet you." **co'o** means "I bid you farewell." Nothing more is needed.

### Practice dialogue

<speaker sprite="lif1">A little later, Lifri introduces herself more fully.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| coi do | Hello (to you). |
| coi do .i mi'e la lifri | Hello. I'm Lifri. |
| coi .i mi'e la mentu | Hello. I'm Mentu. |
| co'o la lifri | Goodbye, Lifri. |
| co'o la mentu | Goodbye, Mentu. |

**New word**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| .i | sentence boundary — separates two sentences (like a spoken full stop) |

> **Grammar note:** **.i** is written with a dot before it. You can chain as many sentences as you like: **coi .i mi'e la mentu .i co'o**.

### Anticipation prompts

> **Say in Lojban:** Hello. *(coi)*

> **Say in Lojban:** I'm Mentu. *(mi'e la mentu)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Goodbye, Lifri. *(co'o la lifri)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Hello. I'm Linto. *(coi .i mi'e la linto)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Hello, Mentu. Goodbye, Mentu. *(coi la mentu .i co'o la mentu)*
