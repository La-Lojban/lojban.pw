## Lesson 19. Son and Daughter

<speaker sprite="lif2">More about family — sons, daughters, boys, girls. The **be** pattern you learned in Lesson 18 keeps working the same way.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| le bersa be mi cu gleki | My son is happy. |
| le tixnu be mi cu zvati le zdani | My daughter is at home. |
| mi se panzi le pa nanla .e le pa nixli | I have a boy and a girl. |
| le tixnu be do cu tavla mi | Your daughter talks to me. |
| xu le bersa be do cu gleki | Is your son happy? |
| go'i | Yes. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| bersa | x₁ is the son of x₂ |
| tixnu | x₁ is the daughter of x₂ |
| nanla | boy |
| nixli | girl |

> **Grammar note:** **be** continues to link possessed items: **le bersa be mi** = "the son of me" = "my son." **le pa nanla** = "the one boy" — numbers can be placed between **le** and the predicate word to specify quantity.
