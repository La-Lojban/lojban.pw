Linto mentions she travelled abroad. They discuss countries and languages — including a new standalone use of **bangu** beyond the **se bangu** you already know.

| <speaker sprite="lin1">go'i .i mi pu litru</speaker> | Yes. I have travelled. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| litru | x₁ travels in/through x₂ |
| gugde | country |
| fi'i | welcome! (attitudinal: hospitality) |
| drata | other, different — **lo drata gugde** = "another country" |
| bangu | language — **bangu** alone names a language; **se bangu** (Lesson 2) = "to speak a language" |

> **Grammar note:**
> **fi'i** is a vocative attitudinal for welcoming someone — like **coi** but warmer: "you are my guest." **drata** means "other / different" and modifies nouns the same way as **barda**: **lo drata gugde** = "another country." Country and language names use **la**: **la .paris.**, **la .lojban.**, etc.
