## Lesson 16. How Much?

<speaker sprite="lif2">Money and shopping. **te vecnu** means "to buy" and **meryru'u** is "US dollar." Prices use **li** for the number — the same **li** you know from clock times.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| .ai mi te vecnu su'o cidja | I'm going to buy some food. |
| xu kargu | Is it expensive? |
| lo nanba cu meryru'u li pa ci | The bread costs 13 US dollars. |
| lo stasu cu meryru'u li re no | The soup costs 20 US dollars. |
| xu do se jdini | Do you have money? |
| na go'i .i mi na se jdini | No. I don't have money. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| te vecnu | to buy (lit. the buyer's role in a sale) |
| su'o | at least one / some (quantifier) — **su'o cidja** = "some food" |
| kargu | expensive |
| jdini | money — **se jdini** = "to have money" |
| meryru'u | x₁ costs x₂ US dollars |

> **Grammar note:** **te vecnu** is a compound — **te** swaps the predicate's slots so the buyer lands in x₁. For now treat **mi te vecnu X** as simply "I buy X." **su'o** before a noun means "some / at least one": **su'o cidja** = "some food." **se jdini** uses the familiar **se** swap: **jdini** = "x₁ is money for x₂", so **se jdini** = "x₁ has money x₂."
