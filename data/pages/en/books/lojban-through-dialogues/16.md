## Lesson 16. How Much?

<speaker sprite="lif2">Money and shopping. **te vecnu** means "to buy" and **meryru'u** is "US dollar." Prices use **li** for the number — the same **li** you know from clock times.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| .ai mi te vecnu su'o da | I'm going to buy something. |
| xu kargu | Is it expensive? |
| ti meryru'u li pa ci | This costs 13 US dollars. |
| ti meryru'u li re no | This costs 20 US dollars. |
| mi na ponse lo jdini | I don't have money. |
| xu do ponse lo jdini | Do you have money? |
| go'i | Yes. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| te vecnu | to buy (lit. the buyer's role in a sale) |
| su'o | at least one / some (quantifier) |
| kargu | expensive |
| jdini | money |
| meryru'u | US dollar(s) |

> **Grammar note:** **te vecnu** is a compound — **te** swaps the predicate's slots so the buyer lands in x₁. For now treat **mi te vecnu X** as simply "I buy X." **su'o da** = "something / at least one thing."
