## Lesson 26. Together

<speaker sprite="lif2">Lifri and Mentu plan a visit together. **jo'u** links people acting side-by-side, and **vitke** / **stali** / **kanpe** round out travel conversation.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| mi jo'u do ba klama le tcadu | You and I will go to the city. |
| .ai mi vitke le pendo | I'm going to visit a friend. |
| mi kanpe lo nu mi stali | I expect to stay. |
| xu do .au vitke mi | Do you want to visit me? |
| go'i .i mi .au vitke do | Yes. I want to visit you. |
| mi jo'u le speni be mi ba klama | My spouse and I will go. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| jo'u | together with (mutual participation — both act in parallel) |
| vitke | x₁ visits x₂ at x₃ |
| stali | x₁ stays / remains at x₂ |
| kanpe | x₁ expects x₂ |

> **Grammar note:** **jo'u** links two people acting side-by-side as equals: **mi jo'u le speni be mi** = "my spouse and I (together)." Compare with **.e** which lists them separately. For travel companions and joint decisions, **jo'u** is the natural choice.
