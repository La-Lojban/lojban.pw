## Lesson 26. Coming Back

Planning the return trip. **di'a** marks resuming or going back; **pe** is a lighter possessive than **be**; and **ba le nu … kei** means "after [event]."

| | Lojban | English |
| --- | -------- | --------- |

| <dialogue-sprite url="/assets/pixra/books/lojban-through-dialogues/icons/bla2.webp" /> | na go'i .i mi ba klama fu le taksi | No. I'll go by taxi. |
| <dialogue-sprite url="/assets/pixra/books/lojban-through-dialogues/icons/bla2.webp" /> | ba le nu vitke kei mi di'a klama le zdani | After the visit, I'll return home. |

| <dialogue-sprite url="/assets/pixra/books/lojban-through-dialogues/icons/bla2.webp" /> | go'i | Yes. |
| <dialogue-sprite url="/assets/pixra/books/lojban-through-dialogues/icons/lif2.webp" /> | le karce pe do cu melbi | Your car is beautiful. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| di'a | resuming / going back to (aspect marker) — **di'a klama** = "return" |
| pe | of / belonging to (loose possessive — lighter than **be**) — **le karce pe do** = "your car" |
| ta | that — near the listener (demonstrative; **ti** = here, **ta** = there, **tu** = yonder) |
| tu | that yonder — far from both speaker and listener |

> **Grammar note:**
> **ba le nu [bridi] kei** = "after [event]." **kei** closes the event-noun started by **nu**: **le nu vitke kei** = "the visiting." **ba le nu vitke kei** = "after the visit." **pe** is a lighter possessive than **be**: **le karce pe do** = "your car." Both **be** and **pe** work for possession; **be** tends to appear inside descriptions, **pe** after complete noun phrases.
