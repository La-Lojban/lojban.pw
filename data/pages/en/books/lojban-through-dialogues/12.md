## Lesson 12. Today and Tomorrow

<speaker sprite="lif2">The three essential day-words: **cabdei** (today), **bavlamdei** (tomorrow), **prulamdei** (yesterday). And **birti** — to be certain.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="lin5">**bavlamdei** — "the day that arrives after" and **prulamdei** — "the day that passed." The words describe themselves!</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| ca le cabdei mi zvati le zdani | Today I'm at home. |
| ca le bavlamdei mi ba klama le panka | Tomorrow I'll go to the park. |
| ca le prulamdei mi pu klama le panka | Yesterday I went to the park. |
| xu do ba klama le panka ca le bavlamdei | Will you go to the park tomorrow? |
| mi na birti | I'm not sure. |
| pei ca le cabdei | How about today? |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| cabdei | today (lit. "current day") |
| bavlamdei | tomorrow (lit. "next-adjacent day") |
| prulamdei | yesterday (lit. "previous-adjacent day") |
| birti | x₁ is certain / sure that x₂ is true |

> **Grammar note:** These compound day-words combine naturally with **pu** / **ba** / **ca**. **ca le prulamdei** = "at yesterday" works like a time phrase. **mi na birti** = "I am not certain" — **na** always comes directly before the predicate.

### Practice dialogue

### Anticipation prompts

> **Say in Lojban:** Today I'm at home. *(ca le cabdei mi zvati le zdani)*

> **Say in Lojban:** I'm not sure. *(mi na birti)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Yesterday I went to the park. *(ca le prulamdei mi pu klama le panka)*

> **Say in Lojban:** How about tomorrow? *(pei ca le bavlamdei)*
