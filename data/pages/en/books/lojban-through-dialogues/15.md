## Lesson 15. Speak Slowly!

<speaker sprite="lif2">A practical lesson for any language learner. Lifri speaks too fast — Mentu needs to slow her down. And the powerful command word **ko**.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| do sutra tavla | You speak fast. |
| ko masno tavla | Speak slowly! |
| ko na sutra tavla | Don't speak fast! |
| mi na djuno | I don't know. |
| xu do djuno | Do you know? |
| na go'i .i ko masno tavla | No. Speak slowly! |
| na go'i .i .e'o do masno tavla | No. Please speak slowly. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ko | you (command form) — replace **do** with **ko** to turn any statement into a command |
| sutra | fast, quick |
| masno | slow |
| djuno | x₁ knows that x₂ is true |
| .e'o | attitudinal: please — polite request; **.e'o do …** = "please, you …" (see [learn-lojban Lesson 1](https://lojban.pw/en/books/learn-lojban/1/) request tables and **muplis-database.tsv** for **.e'o do** lines) |

> **Grammar note:** **ko** is simply **do** with a command force. **do masno tavla** = "you speak slowly (statement)." **ko masno tavla** = "speak slowly! (command)." **ko na** + predicate = "don't…!" **.e'o** softens a request: **.e'o do masno tavla** asks the same thing as **ko masno tavla** but sounds less blunt — good with strangers or teachers.

### Anticipation prompts

> **Say in Lojban:** You speak fast. *(do sutra tavla)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Speak slowly! *(ko masno tavla)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Please speak slowly. *(.e'o do masno tavla)*

> **Say in Lojban:** I don't know. *(mi na djuno)*
