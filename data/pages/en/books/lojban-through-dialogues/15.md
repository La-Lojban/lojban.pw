## Lesson 15. My Family

The conversation turns to family. **se** swaps predicate slots to change perspective; **be** links a possessor inside a noun phrase; **xo** asks "how many?"

| Lojban | English |
| --- | --- |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">xu do speni</speaker> | Are you married? |
| <speaker sprite="bla7">go'i .i le speni be mi cu gleki</speaker> | Yes. My spouse is happy. |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">xu do se panzi</speaker> | Do you have children? |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">go'i .i mi se panzi re</speaker> | Yes. I have two. |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">do se panzi xo</speaker> | How many children do you have? |
| <speaker sprite="bla7">re .i le lanzu be mi cu gleki</speaker> | Two. My family is happy. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| speni | x₁ is the spouse of x₂ |
| panzi | x₁ is the offspring of x₂ |
| lanzu | x₁ is a family including members x₂ |
| se | swaps x₁ and x₂ of a predicate |
| be | links a sumti (noun phrase) inside a description: "of" — **le speni be mi** = "my spouse" |
| xo | how many? (quantity question word — replaces a number) |

> **Grammar note:**
> **se** swaps the first and second slots. **panzi** = "x₁ is offspring of x₂." **se panzi** = "x₁ has offspring x₂." So **mi se panzi re** = "I have two (children)." **be** links a possessor inside a description: **le speni be mi** = "the spouse of me" = "my spouse." **xo** replaces a number in any slot: **do se panzi xo** = "you have how-many children?" Answer by replacing **xo** with a number: **mi se panzi re**.
>
> **Names revisited:** Now that **se** is a tool, you can say **mi se cmene zo lifri** — "I am named by the word *lifri*" — as an alternative to **zo lifri cmene mi** from Lesson 3. Both are correct.
