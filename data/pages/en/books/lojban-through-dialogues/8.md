## Lesson 8. Yesterday and Tomorrow

<speaker sprite="lif2">Mentu wants to talk about what happened yesterday and what will happen tomorrow. Lojban tense markers make this easy.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| mi pu klama le panka | I went to the park. |
| mi ba klama le panka | I will go to the park. |
| do pu tavla ma | Who did you talk to? |
| mi pu tavla la lifri | I talked to Lifri. |
| do ba klama ma | Where will you go? |
| mi ba klama le zdani | I will go home. |
| mi pu klama le panka .i ku'i mi ba klama le zdani | I went to the park, but I'll go home. |
| le panka cu gleki | The park is pleasant. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| pu | past tense — the action happened before now |
| ba | future tense — the action will happen after now |
| ku'i | discursive: but / however — often **.i ku'i** between sentences (see [learn-lojban Lesson 4](https://lojban.pw/en/books/learn-lojban/4/)) |
| cu | separator — marks the end of a noun phrase and the start of the predicate (no translation) |

> **Grammar note:**
> **pu** and **ba** come just before the predicate and shift the time. **mi pu klama** = "I went." **mi ba klama** = "I will go." **cu** is a small structural word — in **le panka cu gleki**, it tells Lojban "the noun phrase is done, here comes the predicate." It carries no meaning of its own. **.i ku'i** links two sentences with a contrast: past action, **but** future plan.
