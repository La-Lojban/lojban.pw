## Lesson 11. I Intend To

<speaker sprite="lif2">Lifri and Mentu are at a cafe deciding what to order. Two key attitudinals: **.ai** (intent) and **pei** (how about?).</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| .ai mi pinxe lo djacu | I'm going to drink water. |
| .au mi citka lo stasu | I want to eat soup. |
| .ai mi citka lo nanba | I'm going to eat bread. |
| pei | How about you? |
| .ai mi pinxe lo djacu | I'm going to drink water too. |
| xu do .au citka lo stasu | Do you want to eat soup? |
| go'i | Yes. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| .ai | I intend to … / I'm going to … (attitudinal: intent) |
| stasu | soup |
| pei | how about …? — turns any attitudinal into a question; standalone: "how do you feel about it?" |

> **Grammar note:** **.ai** expresses intent — something you've decided to do. **.au** (from Lesson 10) expresses desire — something you want. **pei** after an attitudinal asks about the listener's feeling: **.au pei** = "do you want to?"
