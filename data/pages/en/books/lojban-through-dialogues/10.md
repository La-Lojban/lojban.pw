## Lesson 10. I Want to Eat

<speaker sprite="lif2">Mentu is hungry after the walk. Time to learn about eating, drinking, and wanting things.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| .au mi citka | I want to eat. |
| .au mi pinxe | I want to drink. |
| .au mi pinxe lo djacu | I want to drink some water. |
| .au mi citka lo nanba | I want to eat some bread. |
| xu do .au citka | Do you want to eat? |
| go'i | Yes. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| citka | x₁ eats x₂ |
| pinxe | x₁ drinks x₂ |
| .au | I want … / I wish … (attitudinal: desire) |
| lo | some / a(n) — refers to something in general, not a specific one |
| djacu | water |
| nanba | bread |

> **Grammar note:** **.au** at the start of a sentence colours its meaning with desire. **lo** is a general article: **lo djacu** = "some water / water in general." Compare with **le** which points at something specific: **le djacu** = "the water (that particular one)."

### Practice dialogue

<speaker sprite="bla1">Mentu and Lifri discuss what they want.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| .au mi citka | I want to eat. |
| xu do .au pinxe lo djacu | Do you want to drink water? |
| go'i .i .au mi pinxe lo djacu | Yes. I want to drink water. |
| .au mi citka lo nanba | I want to eat bread. |
| .ui | Great! |

### Anticipation prompts

> **Say in Lojban:** I want to eat. *(.au mi citka)*

> **Say in Lojban:** I want to drink water. *(.au mi pinxe lo djacu)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Do you want to eat? *(xu do .au citka)*

> **Say in Lojban:** I want to eat bread. *(.au mi citka lo nanba)*
