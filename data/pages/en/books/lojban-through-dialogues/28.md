## Lesson 28. Coming Back

<speaker sprite="lif2">Planning the return trip. **di'a** means "resuming / going back." Combined with **klama** it gives you "return." Also: **pe** (loose possessive), **fu** (by means of), and **ba le nu…kei** (after an event).</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| mi ba klama le tcadu | I'll go to the city. |
| xu do ba klama fu le karce pe do | Will you go in your own car? |
| na go'i .i mi ba klama fu le taksi | No. I'll go by taxi. |
| ba le nu vitke kei mi di'a klama le zdani | After the visit, I'll return home. |
| xu do ba di'a klama ti | Will you come back here? |
| go'i | Yes. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| di'a | resuming / going back to (aspect marker) |
| pe | of / belonging to (loose possessive — lighter than **be**) |
| fu | by means of / using (marks the transport/method slot) |
| ba le nu … kei | after (something happens) — "after [event]" |

> **Grammar note:** **le nu [bridi] kei** is a closed event-noun: **le nu vitke kei** = "the event of visiting." The **kei** closes it cleanly. **ba le nu vitke kei** = "after the visit." **pe** is a lighter possessive than **be**: **le karce pe do** = "your car."
