## Lesson 24. How Far? How Many?

<speaker sprite="lif2">Planning the route to Lifri's city. **xo** is the quantity question word — "how many?" And distance vocabulary: **sepli** (separated/apart), **jibni** (near).</speaker>

<speaker sprite="lin1">So **xo** replaces a number in a sentence?</speaker>

<speaker sprite="lif5">Exactly — **xo** slots in where a number would go.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| do se panzi xo | How many children do you have? |
| mi se panzi re | I have two. |
| le tcadu cu sepli mi lo ki'otre be li mu no | The city is 50 kilometres from me. |
| le panka cu jibni | The park is near. |
| le tcadu cu darno | The city is far. |
| .ue | Oh, what a surprise! |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| xo | how many? (quantity question word — replaces a number) |
| .ue | surprise! (attitudinal: amazement) |
| sepli | x₁ is separated from x₂ by distance x₃ |
| jibni | x₁ is near x₂ |
| ki'otre | kilometre |

> **Grammar note:**
> **xo** replaces a number in any slot: **do se panzi xo** = "you have how-many children?" Answer by replacing **xo** with a number: **mi se panzi re**. **.ue** expresses surprise — like "Wow!" or "Oh!"
