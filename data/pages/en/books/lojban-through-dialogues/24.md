## Lesson 24. How Far? How Many?

Walking around the city together. Distances, quantities, and two new words that describe what you see.

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| melbi | x₁ is beautiful / pretty to x₂ |
| xamgu | x₁ is good / beneficial |
| sepli | x₁ is separated from x₂ by distance x₃ — **le tcadu cu sepli mi lo ki'otre be li N** = "the city is N km from me" |
| ki'otre | kilometre — **lo ki'otre be li N** = "N kilometres" |

> **Grammar note:**
> **sepli** takes three sumti: the two things separated, and the measure of distance. **lo ki'otre be li mu no** = "50 kilometres" — **ki'otre** (kilometre) + **be li N** to attach the number. **melbi** and **xamgu** are property predicates: **le panka cu melbi** = "the park is beautiful," **le zdani cu xamgu** = "the home is good."
