## Lesson 20. Where Do You Live?

<speaker sprite="lif2">Lifri invites Mentu to visit. Where do you live? How big is your city?</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| do xabju ma | Where do you live? |
| mi xabju lo barda tcadu | I live in a big city. |
| le zdani be mi cu cmalu | My home is small. |
| le tcadu cu barda | The city is big. |
| xu le panzi be do cu xabju le zdani be do | Do your children live in your home? |
| go'i .i le panzi be mi cu xabju le zdani be mi | Yes. My children live in my home. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| xabju | x₁ inhabits / lives in x₂ |
| tcadu | city |
| barda | big, large |
| cmalu | small |

> **Grammar note:** When two predicate words appear side by side — **barda tcadu** — the left modifies the right. This is a **tanru** (compound predicate). **barda tcadu** = "big city." The right-hand word is the head: it's still a city, just a big one.
