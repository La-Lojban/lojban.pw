## Lesson 6. Where Are You?

<speaker sprite="lif2">Lifri, Mentu, and Linto are making plans. They need to say where things are and where they're going — and a quick **ma nuzba** is a natural opener.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| ma nuzba | What's the news? / What's new? |
| mi nelci le panka | I like the park. (casual reply) |
| mi zvati le zdani | I'm at home. |
| do zvati ma | Where are you? |
| mi zvati le panka | I'm at the park. |
| mi klama le panka | I go to the park. |
| do klama ma | Where do you go? |
| mi klama le zdani | I go home. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| zvati | x₁ is present at / located at x₂ |
| le | the — marks a specific thing the speaker has in mind |
| zdani | home, dwelling |
| klama | x₁ goes to x₂ (from x₃ via x₄ by means x₅) |
| panka | park |
| nuzba | news; **ma nuzba** = "what's the news?" — a practical greeting-style question |

> **Grammar note:**
> **le** is like the English "the" — it marks something specific that the speaker has in mind. **le panka** = "the park (I'm thinking of)." For now, treat it as "the." **ma nuzba** is a high-frequency fixed question ("what's the news?" / "what's up?") — treat it as one unit for now, like **ma cmene do** in the previous lesson.
