## Lesson 6. At the Restaurant

<speaker sprite="koc2">This lesson introduces **location questions** with **bu'u ma** (where?), and the important **.aunai** — "I don't want to."</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor4">I feel like I've been eating and drinking for three lessons straight. When do we get to grammar?</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc4">This *is* the grammar. You already know tense markers, attitudinals, articles, question words, and the verb-first sentence shape — you just learned them through soup and juice.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| .au pei do ca citka | Would you like to eat now? |
| .au mi ca citka | I'd like to eat now. |
| .aunai .i ba za go'i | I don't want to. I'll eat in a while. |
| .ai mi pinxe | I'm going to drink something. |
| bu'u ma | Where? |
| mi na djuno | I don't know. |
| .ai mi klama lo gusta | I'm going to go to some restaurant. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| .aunai | I don't want to |
| go'i | (repeat of the previous sentence's predicate — here: eat) |
| bu'u | at (location tense marker) |
| bu'u ma | where? |
| gusta | restaurant |

<speaker sprite="sor5">So **.aunai .i ba za go'i** = "I don't want to. In a while I will [do the same thing again]." **go'i** is an incredible shortcut.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc5">It echoes the last predicate. Very natural in rapid Lojban conversation.</speaker>

### Written prompts

> **Say in Lojban:** I don't want to eat now. *(.aunai mi ca citka)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Where? *(bu'u ma)*

> **Say in Lojban:** I'm going to go to some restaurant. *(.ai mi klama lo gusta)*

### Recycle — Lessons 3–5

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|

| .ai mi pinxe lo jisra ca le vanci | I'm going to drink some juice in the evening. |
