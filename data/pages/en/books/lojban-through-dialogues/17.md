## Lesson 17. Where Do You Live?

Lifri invites Mentu to visit her city. First she asks where he lives — and introduces near vs far.

| Lojban | English |
| --- | --- |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">do xabju ma</speaker> | Where do you live? |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">mi xabju lo barda tcadu</speaker> | I live in a big city. |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">le zdani be mi cu cmalu</speaker> | My home is small. |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">le tcadu cu barda</speaker> | The city is big. |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">le panka cu jibni</speaker> | The park is near. |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">le tcadu cu darno</speaker> | The city is far. |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">xu le panzi be do cu xabju le zdani be do</speaker> | Do your children live in your home? |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">go'i</speaker> | Yes. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| xabju | x₁ inhabits / lives in x₂ |
| tcadu | city |
| barda | big, large |
| cmalu | small |
| jibni | x₁ is near x₂ |
| darno | x₁ is far from x₂ |

> **Grammar note:**
> When two predicate words appear side by side — **barda tcadu** — the left modifies the right. This is a **tanru** (compound predicate). **barda tcadu** = "big city." The right-hand word is the head: it's still a city, just a big one. **jibni** and **darno** can take a second sumti: **le panka cu jibni le zdani** = "the park is near the home."
