## Lesson 17. Cookies and Juice

<speaker sprite="lif2">Shopping continued — Mentu buys multiple items. The connector **.e** lets you link things together.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| .ai mi te vecnu lo titnanba .e lo jisra | I'm going to buy cookies and juice. |
| .ai mi klama lo zarci | I'm going to go to a shop. |
| xu le jisra cu kargu | Is the juice expensive? |
| na kargu .i tolkargu | Not expensive. Cheap. |
| le cidja cu mutce kargu | The food is very expensive. |
| lo nanba cu mutce tolkargu | The bread is very cheap. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| .e | and — connects two noun phrases: **A .e B** = "A and B" |
| zarci | shop, store |
| tolkargu | cheap (lit. opposite-of-expensive) |
| mutce | very, a lot |

> **Grammar note:** **.e** links noun phrases: **lo titnanba .e lo jisra** = "cookies and juice." The prefix **tol-** means "the opposite of" — it's a systematic way to derive antonyms. **tolkargu** = not-expensive = cheap.
