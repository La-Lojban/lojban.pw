## Lesson 7. Good Morning

<speaker sprite="lif2">The next morning. Lifri and Mentu meet again. Time-of-day words make every conversation more natural.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| cerni coi | Good morning! |
| donri coi | Good day! / Hello (daytime)! |
| cerni coi .i do mo | Good morning! How are you? |
| mi gleki | I'm happy. |
| ca le cerni mi zvati le zdani | In the morning I'm at home. |
| ca le vanci mi klama le panka | In the evening I go to the park. |
| ca le nicte mi zvati le zdani | At night I'm at home. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| cerni | morning |
| vanci | evening |
| nicte | night |
| donri | daytime / daylight period — **donri coi** = "good day!" (same pattern as **cerni coi**; see [learn-lojban Lesson 1](https://lojban.pw/en/books/learn-lojban/1/) greetings) |
| ca | at (time) — "now" or "at the time of" |
| gleki | x₁ is happy about x₂ |

> **Grammar note:**
> **ca le cerni** means "at the morning / in the morning." **ca** places the sentence at a particular time. It stacks naturally: **ca le vanci mi klama** = "in the evening I go." **donri coi** pairs **donri** (daytime) with **coi** exactly like **cerni coi** — a natural daytime greeting from fluent-style materials (**dictionary-with-examples.tsv** has example bridi with **donri**).
