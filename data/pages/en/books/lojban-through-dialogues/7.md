## Lesson 7. Good Morning

Morning of the meetup. Mentu and Lifri text each other, compare their plans for the day.

| Lojban | English |
| --- | --- |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">cerni coi .i do mo</speaker> | Good morning! How are you? |
| <speaker sprite="lif5">mi gleki .i donri coi</speaker> | I'm happy. Good day! |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">ca le cerni mi zvati le zdani .i do mo</speaker> | In the morning I'm at home. How about you? |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">go'i .i ca le cerni mi zvati le zdani</speaker> | Same — in the morning I'm at home. |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">ca le vanci mi klama le panka</speaker> | In the evening I'll go to the park. |
| <speaker sprite="lif5">.ui .i ca le nicte mi zvati le zdani</speaker> | Yay! At night I'm at home. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| cerni | morning |
| vanci | evening |
| nicte | night |
| donri | daytime / daylight period — **donri coi** = "good day!" |
| ca | at (time) — "now" or "at the time of" |
| gleki | x₁ is happy about x₂ |

> **Grammar note:**
> **ca le cerni** means "at the morning / in the morning." **ca** places the sentence at a particular time. It stacks naturally: **ca le vanci mi klama** = "in the evening I go." **donri coi** pairs **donri** (daytime) with **coi** exactly like **cerni coi** — a natural daytime greeting.
