## Lesson 3. Thank You

<speaker sprite="lif2">Mentu thanks Lifri for the last lesson. Then Linto, a friend of Lifri's, drops by for the first time.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| ki'e | Thank you. |
| je'e | You're welcome. / Got it. |
| ki'e do | Thank you (to you). |
| je'e do | Sure. / No problem. |
| .ui | Yay! / Great! |
| .u'u | I'm sorry. / Oops. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ki'e | thank you (attitudinal: gratitude) |
| je'e | understood / you're welcome / roger |
| .ui | yay! / hooray! (attitudinal: happiness) |
| .u'u | I'm sorry / oops (attitudinal: repentance) |

> **Grammar note:** Words like **ki'e**, **.ui**, and **.u'u** are called **attitudinals** — they express the speaker's feeling. Each one is a complete sentence on its own, or you can add **do** to direct it at someone: **ki'e do** = "thank you (to you)."

### Anticipation prompts

> **Say in Lojban:** Thank you. *(ki'e)*

> **Say in Lojban:** I'm sorry. *(.u'u)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Yay! Got it. *(.ui .i je'e)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Thank you (to you). *(ki'e do)*
