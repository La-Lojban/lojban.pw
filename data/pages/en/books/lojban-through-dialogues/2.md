## Lesson 2. Do You Speak Lojban?

<speaker sprite="lif2">Lifri wants to know if Mentu has any Lojban at all. Watch how she asks yes/no questions and how Mentu answers.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| xu do se bangu la .lojban. | Can you speak Lojban? |
| go'i | Yes. |
| na go'i | No. |
| xu do milxe se bangu la .lojban. | Can you speak Lojban a little? |
| go'i .i mi milxe se bangu | Yes. I can speak a little. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| xu | yes/no question marker — put it before any sentence to ask whether it is true |
| se bangu | to speak / know a language (lit. "to be a language-user of") |
| go'i | yes / that's right (echoes the previous sentence as true) |
| na | not — negates what follows |
| milxe | mildly, a little, somewhat |

> **Grammar note:** **xu** at the start of a sentence turns it into a yes/no question. **go'i** by itself means "yes, the last thing said is true." **na go'i** means "no, it isn't." **milxe** softens what follows: **milxe se bangu** = "speaks a language a little."

### Anticipation prompts

> **Say in Lojban:** Can you speak Lojban? *(xu do se bangu la .lojban.)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Yes. I can speak a little. *(go'i .i mi milxe se bangu)*

> **Say in Lojban:** No. *(na go'i)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Can you speak Lojban a little? *(xu do milxe se bangu la .lojban.)*
