## Lesson 2. Do You Speak Lojban?

Lifri wants to know if Mentu has any Lojban at all. Watch how she asks yes/no questions and how Mentu answers — full lines, the way people actually reply, not bare **go'i** floating on its own.


| Lojban | English |
| --- | --- |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">coi do .i xu do se bangu la .lojban.</speaker> | Hi — do you speak Lojban? |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">go'i .i mi milxe se bangu</speaker> | Yes. I speak it a little. |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">xu do milxe se bangu la .lojban.</speaker> | Only a little Lojban? |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">go'i</speaker> | Yes — still just a little. |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">xu do na se bangu la .lojban.</speaker> | (teasing) So you don't speak Lojban? |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">na go'i</speaker> | No — that's not true. |


**New words**


| Lojban   | Meaning                                                                       |
| -------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| mi       | I / me                                                                        |
| xu       | yes/no question marker — put it before any sentence to ask whether it is true |
| se bangu | to speak / know a language (lit. "to be a language-user of")                  |
| go'i     | yes / that's right (echoes the previous sentence as true)                     |
| na       | not — negates what follows                                                    |
| milxe    | mildly, a little, somewhat                                                    |


> **Grammar note:**
> **xu** at the start of a sentence turns it into a yes/no question. **go'i** by itself means "yes, the last thing said is true." **na go'i** means "no, it isn't." **milxe** softens what follows: **milxe se bangu** = "speaks a language a little." **mi** is the first-person sumti — you already know **do** from Lesson 1; **mi** is its mirror. **xu do na se bangu la .lojban.** asks whether the *negative* sentence holds; **na go'i** answers "false" — so you *do* speak Lojban (at least a little). In the practice table, **mi na cliva** uses **cliva** from Lesson 1: **na** sits right before the predicate ("I'm not leaving").
