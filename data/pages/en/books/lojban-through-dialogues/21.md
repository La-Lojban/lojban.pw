## Lesson 21. Travel and Countries

<speaker sprite="lif2">Lifri has travelled widely. Geography vocabulary: countries, cities, the word for "far," and **fi'i** — the hospitality greeting.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| fi'i do | Welcome! |
| xu do pu litru lo drata gugde | Have you travelled in another country? |
| go'i .i mi pu litru | Yes. I have travelled. |
| mi xabju lo barda tcadu | I live in a big city. |
| le tcadu cu darno | The city is far. |
| le tcadu cu barda | The city is big. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| litru | x₁ travels in/through x₂ |
| gugde | country |
| drata | other, different |
| fi'i | welcome! (attitudinal: hospitality) |
| darno | x₁ is far from x₂ |

> **Grammar note:** **fi'i** is a vocative attitudinal for welcoming someone — like **coi** but warmer: "you are my guest." **drata** means "other / different" and modifies nouns the same way as **barda**: **lo drata gugde** = "another country."
