## Lesson 14. What Time Is It?

<speaker sprite="lif2">Lifri teaches the clock. You already know the number words from Lesson 9 — now put them to work.</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| ma ca tcika | What time is it now? |
| ti'u li cacra bu mu | At 5 o'clock. |
| ti'u li cacra bu ze | At 7 o'clock. |
| mi ba citka ti'u li cacra bu ze | I'll eat at 7 o'clock. |
| mi pu klama le panka ti'u li cacra bu vo | I went to the park at 4 o'clock. |
| ti'u li cacra bu pa no | At 10 o'clock. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| tcika | x₁ is the time of day of event x₂ |
| ti'u | at (a clock time) — time-point marker |
| li | introduces a number or numeric expression |
| cacra bu | o'clock (lit. "hour-mark") |

> **Grammar note:**
> The clock formula is **ti'u li cacra bu [number]**. Read it as: "at the time marked by [number] o'clock." **li** tells Lojban that what follows is a number. **cacra bu** = "hour" as a label.
