## Lesson 14. Planning the Week

The trio plans next week's meetup — and every week after that. **birti** confirms you're sure; **ro** says "every"; **jeftu** and **djedi** measure time.

| Lojban | English |
| --- | --- |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">ca le cabdei mi zvati le zdani</speaker> | Today I'm at home. |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">ca le bavlamdei mi ba klama lo zarci</speaker> | Tomorrow I'll go to the shop. |
| <speaker sprite="bla7">mi birti</speaker> | I'm certain. |
| <speaker sprite="lif2">ca ro jeftu mi ba klama le panka</speaker> | Every week I'll go to the park. |
| <speaker sprite="lin1">mi ba zvati le zdani re djedi</speaker> | I'll be at home for two days. |
| <speaker sprite="bla2">ca ro detma'i mi ba pleji</speaker> | Every month I'll pay. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| birti | x₁ is certain / sure about x₂ — **mi birti** = "I'm sure" |
| jeftu | week |
| djedi | day |
| ro | every / all — **ca ro jeftu** = "every week" |
| detma'i | calendar month |

> **Grammar note:**
> **ro** means "every" and can quantify any noun: **ca ro jeftu** = "every week," **ca ro detma'i** = "every month," **ro zdani** = "every home." For multi-day stays or spans: **re djedi** = "two days" and **ci jeftu** = "three weeks" work as time expressions after a verb phrase.
