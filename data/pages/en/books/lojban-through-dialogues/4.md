## Lesson 4. I Talk to You

<speaker sprite="lif2">Lifri shows Mentu how to say things about himself and others — the first full sentences with a subject and a predicate. **ju'i** is a quick way to get someone's attention before you speak (see [learn-lojban Lesson 4](https://lojban.pw/en/books/learn-lojban/4/) and **dictionary-with-examples.tsv** for **ju'i**).</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| ju'i do .i .e'u mi'o tavla | Hey you — let's talk. |
| mi tavla do | I talk to you. |
| do tavla mi | You talk to me. |
| mi'o tavla | We talk (you and I). |
| .e'u mi'o tavla | Let's talk. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| tavla | x₁ talks to x₂ about x₃ in language x₄ |
| mi'o | we — you and I together |
| .e'u | let's … / I suggest … (attitudinal: suggestion) |
| ju'i | vocative: hail / “hey!” — get the listener's attention; often **ju'i do** |

> **Grammar note:** Every Lojban predicate has numbered slots. The first slot (x₁) is the actor; the second (x₂) is the target. Order: **x₁ → predicate → x₂ → …** So **mi tavla do** = "I (x₁) talk-to you (x₂)." Swap them and the meaning reverses: **do tavla mi** = "you talk to me." **ju'i** works like **coi** — it expects a following sumti (**ju'i do** = "hey, you"). Then **.i** starts the next sentence.

### Anticipation prompts

> **Say in Lojban:** Hey — let's talk. *(ju'i do .i .e'u mi'o tavla)*

> **Say in Lojban:** I talk to you. *(mi tavla do)*

> **Say in Lojban:** You talk to me. *(do tavla mi)*

> **Say in Lojban:** Let's talk. *(.e'u mi'o tavla)*

> **Say in Lojban:** We talk. *(mi'o tavla)*
