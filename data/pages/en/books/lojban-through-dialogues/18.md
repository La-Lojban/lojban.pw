## Lesson 18. My Family

<speaker sprite="lif2">The conversation turns to family — in the practice dialogue, **Linto** asks Mentu the personal questions and keeps asking until Mentu answers. A powerful new tool: **se** (which swaps predicate slots) and **be** (which links possessors).</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| xu do speni | Are you married? |
| go'i .i le speni be mi cu gleki | Yes. My spouse is happy. |
| xu do se panzi | Do you have children? |
| go'i .i mi se panzi re | Yes. I have two. |
| le lanzu be mi cu gleki | My family is happy. |
| .ui | Wonderful! |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| speni | x₁ is the spouse of x₂ |
| panzi | x₁ is the offspring of x₂ |
| lanzu | x₁ is a family including members x₂ |
| se | swaps x₁ and x₂ of a predicate |
| be | links a sumti (noun phrase) inside a description: "of" |

> **Grammar note:**
> **se** swaps the first and second slots. **panzi** = "x₁ is offspring of x₂." **se panzi** = "x₁ has offspring x₂." So **mi se panzi re** = "I have two (children)." **be** links a possessor inside a description: **le speni be mi** = "the spouse of me" = "my spouse."
>
> **Names again:**
> In Lesson 5 you learned **zo lifri cmene mi** ("the word *lifri* names me"). Now that **se** is a tool, you can say the same thing as **mi se cmene zo lifri** — "I am named by the word *lifri*" (see [CLL Example 19.80](/en/books/cll/19/#ex-19-80) and **dictionary-with-examples.tsv** under **cmene**). Both forms are good Lojban; **zo … cmene mi** stays the shortest pattern for beginners.
