## Lesson 18. My Family

<speaker sprite="lif2">Linto joins the group. The conversation turns to family. A powerful new tool: **se** (which swaps predicate slots) and **be** (which links possessors).</speaker>

| Lojban | English |
|--------|---------|
| xu do se panzi su'o da | Do you have children? |
| go'i .i mi se panzi re da | Yes. I have two children. |
| le lanzu be mi cu barda | My family is big. |
| le speni be mi cu gleki | My spouse is happy. |
| xu do se panzi | Do you have children? |
| na go'i | No. |

**New words**

| Lojban | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| speni | x₁ is the spouse of x₂ |
| panzi | x₁ is the offspring of x₂ |
| lanzu | x₁ is a family including members x₂ |
| se | swaps x₁ and x₂ of a predicate |
| be | links a sumti (noun phrase) inside a description: "of" |

> **Grammar note:** **se** swaps the first and second slots. **panzi** = "x₁ is offspring of x₂." **se panzi** = "x₁ has offspring x₂." So **mi se panzi re da** = "I have two children." **be** links a possessor inside a description: **le speni be mi** = "the spouse of me" = "my spouse."
