---

<speaker sprite="koc1">In Lojban, “part of speech” lines up with **morphology** — you can often tell the type from spelling:</speaker>
- **brivla** (“predicate word”): ignoring **'** and **y**, there is a **consonant cluster in the first five letters**, and the word **ends in a vowel**.
- **cmavo** (“structure word”): **no consonants except at the beginning**, no consonant cluster, **ends in a vowel**.
- **cmene** (“name”): **ends in a consonant**; in modern usage names are usually wrapped in **dots**.

Here **consonant** does **not** include the apostrophe (the **h** sound).

|        | Gloss      | ends in | cluster in first 5 letters | consonants outside initial? |
|--------|------------|---------|----------------------------|-----------------------------|
| brivla | predicate  | vowel   | yes                        | yes                         |
| cmavo  | particle   | vowel   | no                         | no                          |
| cmene  | name       | consonant | free                     | yes                         |

<speaker sprite="sor2">A quick **decision path** (same idea as a flowchart, in text):</speaker>
1. **Does the word end in a consonant?** → treat it as **cmene** (a name; in modern usage names are usually wrapped in **dots**).
2. **Else** (it ends in a vowel): **ignoring `'` and `y`, is there a consonant cluster in the first five letters?** → **brivla** (predicate word).
3. **Else** → **cmavo** (structure word): no internal consonants, so cmavo can be run together without spaces.

<speaker sprite="koc5">Right. The two big checks are **“does it end in a consonant?”** and **“is there a cluster in the first five letters?”**</speaker>
Because **cmavo** never have internal consonants, you can **run them together without spaces**: **pu zi ze'u ri'a vi ve'a** can be written **puzize'uri'avive'a**.

<speaker sprite="sor2">Saves space — but you have to check whether you’re looking at one weird cmavo or a chain of familiar ones.</speaker>
---

<speaker sprite="koc2">**Stress, revisited** — it’s basically this:</speaker>
- **brivla** and **cmene**: second-to-last syllable (**y** doesn’t count as a syllable for this).
- **cmavo**: **free** — but if a cmavo sits right before a **brivla**, you usually **don’t** stress that cmavo.

<speaker sprite="sor2">Stress freedom! Cmavo rules are fiddly — but “don’t stress before the predicate” is a simple default.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">For **cmene** there’s also an exception: you can mark stress with **capital letters** instead of second-to-last — but for now **“second-to-last syllable”** is enough.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor3">We’ll stick to the simple rule — details later!</speaker>
