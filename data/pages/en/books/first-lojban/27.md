## Lesson 27. (Extra) Propositions, properties, relations

<speaker sprite="koc1">Welcome to the **back alley** — a short **philosophy / logic gym** adjacent to Lojban.</speaker>

### Propositions

Many sentences in different languages can **mean the same thing** (“I am human”, **.i mi remna**, *Mi estas homo*, …). That shared content is a **proposition**; any declarative expressing it is a **propositional sentence**.

**du'u** wraps a bridi into **“the proposition that …”**:

<dl class="drani">
<dt>du'u [bridi]</dt>
<dd>x1 is the proposition expressed by [bridi] under textual form x2 …</dd>
</dl>


Philosophers disagree what propositions **are** — ideas, possible worlds, behaviour-dispositions… For Lojban pedagogy: **“content of a declarative bridi.”**

### **ce'u** — open place in a property

**ce'u remna** is **not** a complete bridi by itself — it’s a **property** (“to be human”) with a **slot** marked by **ce'u** (introductory texts often relate this to **ka** abstractions — continue in CLL / BPFK notes).

### Exercises (from Japanese edition)

- Build **du'u**-bridi pairing the same proposition across languages using **zoi** delimiters.  
- Express “the English proposition = the Japanese proposition” using **du** (lesson 15).

### Relatives refresh

**ti noi ke'a remna ku'o cu melbi** — “This, which is human, is beautiful.” Connect **properties** to **poi/noi** practice.
