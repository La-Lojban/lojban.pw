### Ⅲ. Mark each statement _T_ (true) or _F_ (false).

**(1)** The following schema is correct: `zo'e [selbri] [sumti] [sumti] zo'e zo'e` = `[selbri] [sumti] [sumti]`

**(2)** The modification structure of `{blanu kanla mlatu}` is `((blanu kanla) mlatu)`.

**(3)** `{lo sipna ku}` means "sleep" / "the act of sleeping."

**(4)** `{lo se citka ku}` is a sumti meaning "something edible / something that is eaten."

**(5)** In `{mi klama fi ti ta}`, `{ta}` occupies x<sub>2</sub>.

**(6)** When building compound tense tags, the correct order is: PU class · ZEhA class · ZI class.

**(7)** Aspect tags (ZAhO), like spatial distance tags, are optional grammatical elements — they do not have to be stated explicitly.

**(8)** If `{A du B}`, then `{A me B}` is necessarily also true.

**(9)** `{ma}` is a question word placed in the sumti position; `{mo}` is a question word placed in the selbri position.

**(10)** `{e'i}` means "constraint / obligation," and `{e'inai}` means "prohibition / forbidden."

**(11)** In the relative clause `{poi mi klama fi tu}`, `{ke'a}` is assumed to occupy x<sub>2</sub> of `{klama}`.

**(12)** `{.ija}` is a sentence connective meaning "at least one of the two — the antecedent or the consequent" (inclusive or).
