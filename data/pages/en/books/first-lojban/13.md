## Lesson 13. Aspect (event contours)

<speaker sprite="koc1">Continuing our look at context tags, let's talk about _aspect_ (or **ZAhO** cmavo). This tells you which _phase_ of an event you're referring to.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Which phase? You mean like... stages?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Think about the act of _running_:

- The state _before_ you start.
- The moment you _begin_.
- The act while it is _ongoing_.
- The moment you _stop_.
- The moment you _finish_.
- The state _after_ you've finished.

Lojban has a specific tag for each of these "slices" of an event.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor1">I see. So for something like "eating an apple," we could talk about being about to eat, starting to eat, being in the middle of eating, stopping, finishing, or having already eaten.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Exactly! And just like tense tags, these _aspect tags_ go _immediately before the_ _selbri_.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>pu'o</dt><dd>prospective—_about to_ happen</dd>
<dt>co'a</dt><dd>inceptive—the _start_ of the act</dd>
<dt>ca'o</dt><dd>progressive—the act is _ongoing_</dd>
<dt>co'u</dt><dd>cessative—the act has _stopped_ (perhaps prematurely)</dd>
<dt>mo'u</dt><dd>completive—the act is successfully _finished_</dd>
<dt>ba'o</dt><dd>perfective—the act is _done_ / in the aftermath</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor7">Right — it really is the tense follow-up: tags go _before the_ _selbri_. So my try at the pattern would be:</speaker>

> **mi pu'o citka lo plise**
> _I’m about to eat the apple._

> **mi co'a citka lo plise**
> _I begin eating the apple._

> **mi ca'o citka lo plise**
> _I’m eating the apple._

> **mi co'u citka lo plise**
> _I finish eating the apple._

> **mi ba'o citka lo plise**
> _I’ve already eaten the apple._


<speaker sprite="koc2">You can also _combine tense and aspect_! The standard order is tense first, then aspect.</speaker>

> **mi pu zi ba'o citka lo plise**
> _A little while ago, I had just finished eating the apple._


### Exercise

- Lojban for:
  1. Sora is about to go to sleep now.
  2. Koshon is in the middle of drinking water from the river.
  3. That beautiful bird flew away long ago.
  4. That man just died. (Hint: treat "dying" as the "cessation of living".)

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>sipna</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> sleeps</dd>
<dt>pinxe</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> drinks x<sub>2</sub> from x<sub>3</sub></dd>
<dt>rirxe</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a river …</dd>
<dt>djacu</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is water</dd>
<dt>melbi</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is beautiful …</dd>
<dt>cipni</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a bird …</dd>
<dt>cliva</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> leaves x<sub>2</sub> …</dd>
<dt>jmive</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is alive …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor5">Alright, let's try these!
1: **la .soran. ca pu'o sipna**
2: **la .kocon. ca'o pinxe lo djacu ku lo rirxe ku**
3: **le melbi cipni pu zu ba'o cliva**
4: Wait, "That man over there"... is that **vu nanmu** (far man)? So, **lo vu nanmu ku**?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Spot on. **lo vu nanmu ku** means "the distant man" or "that man over there."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">Then 4 is: **lo vu nanmu pu zi co'u jmive**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Exactly! Another nice side benefit: putting _tense or aspect tags_ before a _selbri_ also _marks the start of the verb_, acting much like the **cu** we learned earlier. It prevents "accidental _tanru_" from forming.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">Oh, cool! So if a _selbri_ is tagged, it can't be mistakenly swallowed as part of a compound word on its left.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Correct. And just like before, once you've built your tagged _selbri_, you can wrap the whole thing in **lo … ku** to turn it into a description.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">So we can basically nominalize an entire phased event. That's powerful!</speaker>
