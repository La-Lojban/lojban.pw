## Lesson 13. Aspect (event contours)

<speaker sprite="koc1">Continuing our look at context tags, let's talk about **aspect** (or **ZAhO** cmavo). This tells you which **phase** of an event you're referring to.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor3">Which phase? You mean like... stages?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">Think about the act of **running**:

- The state **before** you start.
- The moment you **begin**.
- The act while it is **ongoing**.
- The moment you **stop**.
- The moment you **finish**.
- The state **after** you've finished.

Lojban has a specific tag for each of these "slices" of an event.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor1">I see. So for something like "eating an apple," we could talk about being about to eat, starting to eat, being in the middle of eating, stopping, finishing, or having already eaten.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Exactly! And just like tense tags, these **aspect tags** go **immediately before the selbri**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>pu'o</dt><dd>prospective—**about to** happen</dd>
<dt>co'a</dt><dd>inceptive—the **start** of the act</dd>
<dt>ca'o</dt><dd>progressive—the act is **ongoing**</dd>
<dt>co'u</dt><dd>cessative—the act has **stopped** (perhaps prematurely)</dd>
<dt>mo'u</dt><dd>completive—the act is successfully **finished**</dd>
<dt>ba'o</dt><dd>perfective—the act is **done** / in the aftermath</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor2">Right — it really is the tense follow-up: tags go **before the selbri**. So my try at the pattern would be:</speaker>

> mi **pu'o** citka lo plise
> _I’m about to eat the apple._

> mi **co'a** citka lo plise
> _I begin eating the apple._

> mi **ca'o** citka lo plise
> _I’m eating the apple._

> mi **co'u** citka lo plise
> _I finish eating the apple._

> mi **ba'o** citka lo plise
> _I’ve already eaten the apple._


<speaker sprite="koc5">You can also **combine tense and aspect**! The standard order is tense first, then aspect.</speaker>

> mi **pu zi ba'o** citka lo plise
> _A little while ago, I had just finished eating the apple._


### Exercise

- Lojban for:
  1. Sora is about to go to sleep now.
  2. Koshon is in the middle of drinking water from the river.
  3. That beautiful bird flew away long ago.
  4. That man just died. (Hint: treat "dying" as the "cessation of living".)

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>sipna</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> sleeps</dd>
<dt>pinxe</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> drinks x<sub>2</sub> from x<sub>3</sub></dd>
<dt>rirxe</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a river …</dd>
<dt>djacu</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is water</dd>
<dt>melbi</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is beautiful …</dd>
<dt>cipni</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a bird …</dd>
<dt>cliva</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> leaves x<sub>2</sub> …</dd>
<dt>jmive</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is alive …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">Alright, let's try these!
1: **la .soran. ca pu'o sipna**
2: **la .kocon. ca'o pinxe lo djacu ku lo rirxe ku**
3: **le melbi cipni pu zu ba'o cliva**
4: Wait, "That man over there"... is that **vu nanmu** (far man)? So, **lo vu nanmu ku**?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Spot on. **lo vu nanmu ku** means "the distant man" or "that man over there."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">Then 4 is: **lo vu nanmu pu zi co'u jmive**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Exactly! Another nice side benefit: putting **tense or aspect tags** before a selbri also **marks the start of the verb**, acting much like the **cu** we learned earlier. It prevents "accidental tanru" from forming.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Oh, cool! So if a selbri is tagged, it can't be mistakenly swallowed as part of a compound word on its left.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Correct. And just like before, once you've built your tagged selbri, you can wrap the whole thing in **lo ... ku** to turn it into a description.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So we can basically nominalize an entire phased event. That's powerful!</speaker>
