## Lesson 13. Aspect (event contours)

<speaker sprite="koc1">Continuation: **aspect** — which **phase** of an event you mean (also called **ZAhO**).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor3">Um…</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">Think **running**:</speaker>

- before starting  
- starting  
- ongoing  
- stopping  
- finishing  
- after finishing  

Lojban tags **which slice** of the event.

<speaker sprite="sor1">For “eating an apple”: about to eat, start eating, eating, stop eating, finished eating, already ate.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Yes. **Aspect tags** go **before the selbri**, like tense — **aspect** in the broad Lojban sense.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>pu'o</dt><dd>about to / prospective</dd>
<dt>co'a</dt><dd>inceptive — starting</dd>
<dt>ca'o</dt><dd>ongoing / progressive</dd>
<dt>co'u</dt><dd>cessation — stopping</dd>
<dt>mo'u</dt><dd>completive — completing</dd>
<dt>ba'o</dt><dd>perfective — already done / aftermath</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor2">Right — it really is the tense follow-up: tags go **before the selbri**. So my try at the pattern would be:</speaker>

> mi **pu'o** citka lo plise
> _I’m about to eat the apple._

> mi **co'a** citka lo plise
> _I begin eating the apple._

> mi **ca'o** citka lo plise
> _I’m eating the apple._

> mi **co'u** citka lo plise
> _I finish eating the apple._

> mi **ba'o** citka lo plise
> _I’ve already eaten the apple._


<speaker sprite="koc5">**Combine tense + aspect:** tense first, then aspect.</speaker>

> mi **pu zi ba'o** citka lo plise
> _A short time ago I had already eaten the apple._


### Exercise

- Lojban for:
  1. Sora is about to sleep now.  
  2. Koshon is drinking water from the river.  
  3. That beautiful bird left long ago. (use **le** for “that”)  
  4. That man just died. (treat dying as **end of living**.)

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>sipna</dt><dd>x1 sleeps</dd>
<dt>pinxe</dt><dd>x1 drinks x2 from x3</dd>
<dt>rirxe</dt><dd>x1 is a river …</dd>
<dt>djacu</dt><dd>x1 is water</dd>
<dt>melbi</dt><dd>x1 is beautiful …</dd>
<dt>cipni</dt><dd>x1 is a bird …</dd>
<dt>cliva</dt><dd>x1 leaves x2 …</dd>
<dt>jmive</dt><dd>x1 is alive …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">1–3: **la .soran. ca pu'o sipna**; **la .kocon. ca'o pinxe lo djacu ku lo rirxe ku**; **le melbi cipni pu zu ba'o cliva**.  
4: “That man over there” — **vu nanmu** is “far man”; **lo vu nanmu ku**?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Yes — **lo vu nanmu ku** ≈ “that distant man.”</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">4: **lo vu nanmu pu zi co'u jmive**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Right. **Tense/aspect before selbri** also **prevent accidental tanru** — like **cu**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">So tagged selbri don’t get swallowed as tanru pieces.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Right — and a whole tagged selbri can be wrapped in **lo … ku** again.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So we can nominalise the whole thing.</speaker>
