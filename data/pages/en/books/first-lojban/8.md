## Lesson 8. Conversion (SE)

<speaker sprite="koc1">We’ll widen selbri — which, practically, widens **sumti** tricks again.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor3">That sounds familiar…</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">Lesson 8 déjà vu! **SE cmavo** go on a selbri and **swap place x1** with x2, x3, x4, or x5.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>se</dt>
<dd>swap x1 and x2</dd>
<dt>te</dt>
<dd>swap x1 and x3</dd>
<dt>ve</dt>
<dd>swap x1 and x4</dd>
<dt>xe</dt>
<dd>swap x1 and x5</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor5">“Swap”?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Example with **klama**:</speaker>
<dl class="valsi">
<dt>klama</dt>
<dd>x1 goes to x2 from x3 via x4 by means x5</dd>
</dl>

> **mi klama ta** = **ta se klama mi**
> _I go there._

> **mi klama zo'e ti** = **ti te klama zo'e mi**
> _I go from here._

> **mi klama zo'e zo'e tu** = **tu ve klama zo'e zo'e mi**
> _I go via there._

> **mi klama zo'e zo'e zo'e ti** = **ti xe klama zo'e zo'e zo'e mi**
> _I go using this._


<speaker sprite="sor3">So **se** swaps 1–2, **te** 1–3, **ve** 1–4, **xe** 1–5.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">SE only **converts** — they carry no extra meaning.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Worth it?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">When you only care about a later place, it saves **zo'e**:</speaker>

> **[zo'e] klama zo'e zo'e zo'e ti** = **ti xe klama [zo'e] …**
> _(Someone) goes using this._


<speaker sprite="sor5">Hmm…</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc3">But swap + **lo** lets you **nominalise any place**:</speaker>

> **lo klama ku**
> _goer (x1 of klama)_

> **lo se klama ku**
> _destination (x1 of se klama = x2 of klama)_

> **lo te klama ku**
> _origin_

> **lo ve klama ku**
> _route_

> **lo xe klama ku**
> _vehicle / means_


<speaker sprite="sor7">Nice — sumti power-up!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So *that’s* what “practically sumti” meant.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Yup. If confused, remember: **with se/te/ve/xe, lo can extract x2–x5.**</speaker>
