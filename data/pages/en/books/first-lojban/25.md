## Lesson 25. Attitudinals (UI)

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**Attitudinals** are “syntactically sticky but semantically free” — they attach **to the previous word** (or whole structure opened by that word) but express **speaker attitude** toward **something broader** in context.

Examples:

<dl class="drani">
<dt>.a'o .au .ie .iu .ua .ue .ui .u'i</dt>
<dd>hope, desire, agreement, fondness, discovery, surprise, happiness, amusement</dd>
</dl>


> .i **.ui** mi jimpe fi la .lojban.
> _(Yay) I understand about Lojban._

> .i **.a'o** mi mitre li papibi
> _I wish I were 1.8 m tall._

> lo **.iu** vi titla cu kukte
> _This sweet thing ♡ is tasty._


### Intensity **cai / sai / ru'e** and **nai / cu'i**

Rough scale: stronger ↔ weaker ↔ neutral ↔ negative — check dictionary entries for **-nai** / **-cu'i** forms.

### COI — vocatives

**coi, co'o, mi'e, ki'e, je'e, vi'o, di'ai, fau'u** + optional sumti + **do'u**.

> coi la .soran. mi'e la .kocon.
> _Hi Sora, I’m Koshon._

> ki'e lo sidju ― je'e
> _Thanks, helper. ― You’re welcome._


### **pei** again

After an attitudinal: “do you feel that?” Standalone **pei** asks how the hearer feels about the previous **word**.

Consult **selma'o UI** and **COI** in [The Complete Lojban Language](https://lojban.github.io/cll/) or your favourite reference for the full zoo of particles.
