## Lesson 25. Attitudinals (UI)

<speaker sprite="koc1">Finally, we have **attitudinals**. These are "contextual stickers" that attach to a word to express how the speaker feels about it, but they don't change the underlying grammar of the sentence. They're what make Lojban feel "alive."</speaker>

Examples:

<dl class="drani">
<dt>.a'o, .au, .ie, .iu, .ua, .ue, .ui, .u'i</dt>
<dd>hope, desire, agreement, fondness (love), discovery (aha!), surprise, happiness, amusement</dd>
</dl>

> .i **.ui** mi jimpe fi la .lojban.
> _(Yay!) I understand Lojban._

> .i **.a'o** mi mitre li papibi
> _I wish I were 1.8 m tall._

> lo **.iu** vi titla cu kukte
> _This sweet thing (which I'm fond of ♡) is tasty._</speaker>

You can adjust the strength of your emotion by adding words like **cai** (intense), **sai** (strong), or **ru'e** (slight). You can also use **nai** to flip the meaning (like from happy to sad) or **cu'i** for a neutral feeling.

### COI — vocatives

**coi, co'o, mi'e, ki'e, je'e, vi'o, di'ai, fau'u** + optional sumti + **do'u**.

> coi la .soran. mi'e la .kocon.
> _Hello, Sora! I'm Koshon._

> ki'e lo sidju ― je'e
> _Thanks for the help! ― You're welcome._


You can put **pei** after an attitudinal to ask "Do you feel that way too?" A standalone **pei** just asks "How are you feeling about this?"

For the full list of these expressive particles, you can dive into the **UI** and **COI** sections of [The Complete Lojban Language](https://lojban.github.io/cll/) or look at a reference card. There's a whole "zoo" of them to explore!
