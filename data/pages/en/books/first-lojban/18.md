## Lesson 18. Commands and requests

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc2.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

After negation and questions: how to **urge** someone to act — **commands**.

**Replace sumti _do_ with _ko_** in the bridi you want to be imperative.

<dl class="drani">
<dt>ko</dt>
<dd>imperative <em>do</em>; wide politeness range from “do it” to “please do.”</dd>
</dl>


> **ko** stali ta
> _Stay there._

> **ko** na kusru mi
> _Don’t be mean to me._

> lo prenu cu prami **ko**
> _Act so that people love you._


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**ko** can sit in any sumti slot — the command is “make this bridi true.”

---

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

For nuance (request, suggestion, permission, …) use **attitudinals** at **.i**-position:

<dl class="drani">
<dt>.e'o</dt>
<dd>request</dd>
<dt>.e'u</dt>
<dd>suggestion</dd>
<dt>.e'unai</dt>
<dd>warning against</dd>
<dt>.e'a</dt>
<dd>permission</dd>
<dt>.e'anai</dt>
<dd>forbiddance</dd>
<dt>.e'i</dt>
<dd>constraint / obligation</dd>
<dt>.e'inai</dt>
<dd>release / “up to you”</dd>
</dl>


(Plus **pei** for “may I?” patterns — see below.)

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/sor5.png" caption="Sora" definition=""></pixra>

Some sentences keep **do** instead of **ko** — proposals aren’t always imperatives.

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc4.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**Attitudinal + pei** asks whether the hearer holds that attitude:

<dl class="drani">
<dt>pei</dt>
<dd>attitudinal question.</dd>
</dl>


> .e'a pei mi lasna do lo bitmu lo dunja badna
> _May I stick you to the wall with a frozen banana?_

> ― nai
> _No._


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/sor2.png" caption="Sora" definition=""></pixra>

**nai** flips attitudinals — like **to'e** for NAhE, but not identical (details later).
