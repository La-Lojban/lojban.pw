## Lesson 21. Numbers

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

Digits **pa re ci vo mu xa ze bi so no** (1–9, 0) — vowels follow **a e i o u** pattern (except **mu** uses **u**).

Multi-digit: concatenate: **pano** 10, **recivo** 234, **renopamu** 2015.

### **li** — the number as sumti

<dl class="drani">
<dt>li</dt>
<dd>number article</dd>
<dt>lo'o</dt>
<dd>closes <strong>li</strong> (almost always elidable)</dd>
</dl>


> **li xa** pilji **li re li ci**
> _6 is 2×3._

> lo nu le nixli cu limna cu mentu **li reno**
> _The girl swam for 20 minutes._

> lo vi nanba cu grake **li sovo**
> _This bread is 94 grams._

> **li so pi'e cimu** tcika lo nu mi co'a cikna
> _I woke at 9:35._


<dl class="drani">
<dt>pi'e</dt>
<dd>sub-number separator (time, dates)</dd>
</dl>


### Counting **lo/le** sumti

**[article] [number] selbri** ≈ “N somethings …”

> lo **mu** verba cu kelci lo **re** bolci
> _Five children play with two balls._


### **mei**

**[number] mei** → selbri “x1 is a set/cardinality N …” — combine with **noi** for “these N things.”

### More number cmavo

**pi** (decimal point), **ki'o** (thousands separator), **ro/so'a/so'e/so'i/so'o/so'u**, **su'e/su'o/za'u/me'i**, **xo** (how many?).

> lo **xo** prenu cu klama ti
> _How many people come here? — **mu** — Five._
