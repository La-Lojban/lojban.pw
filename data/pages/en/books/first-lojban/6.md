### Chaining brivla builds tanru

<speaker sprite="koc1">**Brivla** (predicate words) can be **chained**; such a chain is a **tanru**.</speaker>
> **blanu kanla mlatu**
> _blue-eyed cat_


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>blanu</dt>
<dd>x1 is blue</dd>
<dt>kanla</dt>
<dd>x1 is an eye of x2</dd>
<dt>mlatu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a cat …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">So the left pieces modify the right?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Important: **tanru grouping is always unambiguous** — Lojban fixes how modification works.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So no attachment ambiguity?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Right — strict rules. The cost: sometimes what you *want* to say isn’t what the grammar *allows* — watch for that.</speaker>
The rule is simple: **each left group modifies the immediate word on its right.**

> **((blanu kanla) mlatu)**
> _((blue eye) cat)_

> **((((stedu xunre) finpe) citka) mlatu)**
> _((((red-headed) fish) eating) cat)_


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>stedu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a head of x2</dd>
<dt>xunre</dt>
<dd>x1 is red</dd>
<dt>finpe</dt>
<dd>x1 is a fish …</dd>
<dt>citka</dt>
<dd>x1 eats x2</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">Japanese “red-headed fish-eating cat” is ambiguous — Lojban picks one tree.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">That’s part of Lojban’s **syntactic precision** — tanru grouping is a big example.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">Lojban’s solid stuff.</speaker>
### Tanru meanings are flexible

<speaker sprite="koc1">Tanru often mean “Y related to X,” “X-ish Y,” etc.</speaker>
> **blanu mlatu**
> _[blue][cat] — could be blue fur, blue eyes, blue collar, … context decides._


<speaker sprite="sor1">Humans fill in the usual reading.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">In ordinary situations, trust your intuition.</speaker>
### A tanru is still a selbri

<speaker sprite="koc1">A tanru acts as **one selbri**. Its **place structure comes from the rightmost brivla**.</speaker>
So **blanu mlatu** and **stedu xunre finpe citka mlatu** both share **mlatu**’s places.

> **ti blanu mlatu**
> _this is some kind of blue cat._

> **tu stedu xunre finpe citka mlatu**
> _that is a red-headed fish-eating cat (species unspecified)._
