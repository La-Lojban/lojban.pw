### Truth questions: **xu**

<speaker sprite="koc1">Good — you still remember **na**. **Truth questions**: put **xu** right after **.i** (or at utterance start): **.i xu …**</speaker>

<dl class="drani">
<dt>xu</dt>
<dd>attitudinal; asks whether the following bridi holds.</dd>
</dl>

> [.i] xu lo vi prenu cu mamta mi
> _Is this person my mother?_

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>mamta</dt>
<dd>x1 is x2’s mother</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">What *is* with this example…? And what’s an **“attitudinal”**?</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc1">**Attitudinals** express attitude — we’ll survey them in the finale. Now: how do you **answer**? Basically **go'i** (“yes, that bridi”) or **na go'i** (“no”).</speaker>

> .i xu lo vi prenu cu mamta mi
> _Is this my mother?_

> ― .i na go'i .i tu mamta do
> _No; **that** one is your mother._

<dl class="drani">
<dt>go'i</dt>
<dd>pro-bridi; repeats the previous bridi.</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">(So she wasn’t your mother after all…) **go'i** means “that’s right” — it **repeats** the last bridi.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc2">Exactly. Grammatically **go'i** is a selbri, so **na go'i** is “that bridi doesn’t hold.” For **question answers**, that’s all you need for now.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor1">Got it. English **yes/no** flips on **negative questions** — how does Lojban handle that?</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc3">Tricky spot — **go'i** echoes the **exact** previous bridi. With **xu … na mamta**, both plain **go'i** and **na go'i** can sound like “not your mother” in English. Use **ja'a go'i** to affirm the **positive** inner bridi when you need clarity.</speaker>

> .i xu lo vi prenu na mamta mi
> _Is this person not my mother?_

> ja'a go'i
> _(affirms the positive inner bridi — roughly “yes, they are your mother.”)_

### Wh- questions: **ma**

<speaker sprite="koc1">**Wh- questions**: put **ma** wherever a **sumti** goes — one or many per sentence. Answer by supplying sumti **in order**.</speaker>

<dl class="drani">
<dt>ma</dt>
<dd>sumti question.</dd>
</dl>

> .i mi ca catlu ma
> _What am I looking at?_

> ― .i lo patfu
> _Father._

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>catlu</dt>
<dd>x1 looks at / watches x2</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc2">**When / where / why / how**: combine a tag with **ma** — **ca ma**, **bu'u ma**, **ki'u ma**, **ta'i ma** (from **tadji**, “by method …”).</speaker>

<dl class="drani">
<dt>ca ma</dt>
<dd>when</dd>
<dt>bu'u ma</dt>
<dd>where (at …)</dd>
<dt>ki'u ma</dt>
<dd>why (with what reason)</dd>
<dt>ta'i ma</dt>
<dd>how (by what method)</dd>
</dl>

### **mo** — selbri question

<speaker sprite="koc1">**mo** replaces the **selbri**: “What is x1 / what is going on?” Answer with a selbri — same idea as **ma**, different slot.</speaker>

<dl class="drani">
<dt>mo</dt>
<dd>selbri question.</dd>
</dl>

> .i lo vi prenu cu mo
> _Who / what (as predicate) is this person?_

> ― .i ja'a patfu
> _Truly father._

<speaker sprite="sor1">So **mo** works like a selbri slot — including inside **tanru** pieces?</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc5">Yes — e.g. **mo nanmu** “what kind of man?” **mo** can sit in tanru pieces (check current usage in reference material).</speaker>
