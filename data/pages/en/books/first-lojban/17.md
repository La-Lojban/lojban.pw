### Truth questions: **xu**

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

Put **xu** right after **.i** (or at utterance start): **.i xu …**

<dl class="drani">
<dt>xu</dt>
<dd>attitudinal; asks whether the following bridi holds.</dd>
</dl>


> [.i] xu lo vi prenu cu mamta mi
> _Is this person my mother?_


<speaker sprite="sor1">What’s “attitudinal”?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">**Attitudinals** express attitude — we’ll survey them in the finale.</speaker>
Answers: **go'i** (“yes, that bridi”) or **na go'i** (“no”).

> .i xu lo vi prenu cu mamta mi
> _Is this my mother?_

> ― .i na go'i .i tu mamta do
> _No; **that** one is your mother._


<dl class="drani">
<dt>go'i</dt>
<dd>pro-bridi; repeats the previous bridi.</dd>
</dl>


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/sor1.png" caption="Sora" definition=""></pixra>

English yes/no flips on negative questions — Lojban?

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc3.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**go'i** repeats the **exact previous bridi**. With **xu … na mamta**, both **go'i** and **na go'i** can map to awkward English — use **ja'a go'i** to affirm the **positive** inner bridi when needed.

> .i xu lo vi prenu na mamta mi
> _Is this person not my mother?_

> ja'a go'i
> _(affirms the positive inner bridi — roughly “yes, they are your mother.”)_


### Wh- questions: **ma**

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

Put **ma** where a sumti goes — one or many **ma** per sentence. Answer by filling sumti in order.

<dl class="drani">
<dt>ma</dt>
<dd>sumti question.</dd>
</dl>


> .i mi ca catlu ma
> _What am I looking at?_

> ― .i lo patfu
> _Father._


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc2.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**When / where / why / how** combos: **ca ma**, **bu'u ma**, **ki'u ma**, **ta'i ma** (with **ta'i** from **tadji**).

### **mo** — selbri question

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**mo** replaces the selbri: “What is x1 / what is happening?” Answer with a selbri.

<dl class="drani">
<dt>mo</dt>
<dd>selbri question.</dd>
</dl>


> .i lo vi prenu cu mo
> _Who / what (as predicate) is this person?_

> ― .i ja'a patfu
> _Truly father._


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc5.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**mo** can appear inside tanru pieces (unlike some older textbook restrictions — check current usage).
