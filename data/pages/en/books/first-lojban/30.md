## Lesson 30. Advanced name words: stress and forbidden clusters

<speaker sprite="sor1">You know what bugged me about Lesson 3? Koshon said cmevla _can_ have stress somewhere other than the second-to-last syllable, and then Sora just yelled "let's ignore that!" and we moved on.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sev1">Classic Sora. Okay, let's fix it. This lesson is about two things: marking non-default stress, and the consonant clusters that are _never_ allowed anywhere in Lojban.</speaker>

### Non-default stress in cmevla

<speaker sprite="sev1">You know the default: <u>stress the second-to-last syllable</u>, ignoring **y**.

But names from other languages don't always fit that pattern. Lojban lets you override it: <u>capitalize the letters of the stressed syllable</u>.</speaker>

> **la .akasakas.** → akaSAkas (stress falls naturally on second-to-last: -sa-)
> **la .aKAsakas.** → AKAsakas (stress on the first syllable, marked explicitly)
> **la .iaTSUxacin.** → iaTSUxacin (八つ橋 — stress on the second syllable)

<speaker sprite="sor2">Just capitalize the whole stressed syllable?</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sev2">Technically it's enough to capitalize just the vowel, but capitalizing the whole syllable is easier to read. Either way is valid.

Some writers also use diacritical marks — like **la .akásakas.** — but that's non-ASCII and not everyone can type it. Capitals work everywhere.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor1">So the two forms **la .akasakas.** and **la .aKAsakas.** represent the same name with different stress placements, not two different words.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sev4">Exactly. It's an accent record, not a spelling distinction.

One practical point: whether to preserve the original accent is up to personal taste. Some people think Lojbanization _should_ change the accent (it's part of adapting the word). Others want to keep the original as close as possible. Both views are reasonable.</speaker>

### Forbidden consonant clusters

<speaker sprite="sev1">Now the important list. Lojban avoids certain sound combinations — usually because they're easy to mishear or because they don't fit the word-boundary system. Here are the key forbidden patterns:</speaker>

<div class="note">

**Never allowed in Lojban (at word junctions or within a word):**

1. _Double consonants_ — `kk`, `dd`, `pp`, `ss` … any consonant repeated back-to-back.
2. _Mixed voice in obstruents_ — e.g. `pv`, `gs`, `td`, `gk` … pairing a voiced obstruent (`b d g v z j`) with an unvoiced one (`p t k f s c x`). (**r l m n** are not obstruents and can mix freely.)
3. _The sibilant cluster_ — `cs`, `sc`, `jz`, `zj` — these sounds are too similar and blend together.
4. _Specific forbidden pairs:_ `cx`, `kx`, `xc`, `xk`, `mz`
5. _Specific forbidden triples:_ `ndj`, `ndz`, `ntc`, `nts`

</div>

<speaker sprite="sor5">That's a lot. Do I have to memorise all of those to make a cmevla?</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sev3">For everyday Lojbanization you mostly need rules 1 and 2 — they cover the vast majority of cases. The others are rarer. And the fix is always the same: <u>insert a **y** between the two problem consonants</u>.</speaker>

> Problem: **London** → `nd` is allowed, but the final `n` would give us **.londyn.** — wait, that's fine actually.
> Problem: **Marx** → `rx` is fine. But **Marx + s** at the end: **.markss.** — double-s! Fix: **.markyss.** or simply **.marks.**

<speaker sprite="sor2">So **y** is the universal lubricant between stuck consonants.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sev2">Exactly. And two more vowel rules to watch when making name words:</speaker>

<div class="note">

_Vowel rules in cmevla:_

- **i** and **u** become semivowels (**y**-glide and **w**-glide) when they appear _after a consonant_ and before another vowel. To avoid this if you don't want it, double the vowel: **giia** for `ぎゃ`, **niio** for `にょ`.
- Any two non-**i**/**u** vowels in a row need an apostrophe between them: **o'u**, **e'a**, etc.

</div>

<speaker sprite="sor1">So "Tokyo" (東京 = Toukyou) would be **.tokiion.** to avoid the **kyo** glide becoming a semivowel?</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sev2">Yep, or **.tokion.** if you're fine with the kyo-glide. Either is defensible. The point is that the parser can still tell the words apart.
