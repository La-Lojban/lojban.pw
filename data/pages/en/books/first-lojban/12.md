## Lesson 12. Tense: time and space

<speaker sprite="koc1">_Tense_ in Lojban is straightforward: just put a **PU**-series word _immediately before the_ **selbri**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>ca</dt><dd>now</dd>
<dt>pu</dt><dd>before (past)</dd>
<dt>ba</dt><dd>after (future)</dd>
</dl>

> mi **ca** citka lo plise
> _I am eating the apple **now**._

> mi **pu** citka lo plise
> _I ate an apple (in the **past**)._

> mi **ba** citka lo plise
> _I will eat an apple (**later**)._


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>citka</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> eats x<sub>2</sub></dd>
<dt>plise</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> is an apple …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">Simple enough. But what do you mean by "tag"?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">**ca**, **pu**, and **ba** are what we call _tags_. They "stick" to the following word and give it a specific time or place context. For now, we'll just stick them onto the **selbri**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">Got it. They're like little context stickers!</speaker>

### Compound tags

<speaker sprite="koc2">You can get even more specific by adding _distance_ words after your time direction: **zi** for "near" and **zu** for "far."</speaker>

<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th></th>
<th><b>zi</b></th>
<th><b>zu</b></th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>pu</td>
<td>a little before</td>
<td>long ago</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>ba</td>
<td>soon</td>
<td>far future</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<dl class="drani">
<dt>zi</dt><dd>short distance in time (after PU)</dd>
<dt>zu</dt><dd>long distance in time (after PU)</dd>
</dl>

> mi **pu zi** citka lo plise
> _I ate an apple a short while ago._

> mi **ba zu** citka lo plise
> _I’ll eat an apple in the distant future._


<speaker sprite="sor2">Direction plus distance—that's a neat little system.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">That creates a _compound tag_. And we can take it even further by adding _duration_: **ze'i** for a short duration and **ze'u** for a long one.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>ze'i</dt><dd>short extent of interval</dd>
<dt>ze'u</dt><dd>long extent of interval</dd>
</dl>

> mi **pu ze'i** bajra
> _I ran for a short time in the past._

> mi **ba zi ze'u** bajra
> _I'll soon be running for a long time._


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>bajra</dt><dd>x<sub>1</sub> runs on x<sub>2</sub> …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor6">So the order goes: _Direction → Distance → Duration_. I think I can remember that.</speaker>

### Space: FAhA, VA, VEhA

<speaker sprite="koc1">The exact same pattern works for _space_ too! In Lojban, time and space use the same parallel structure (we call both "tense" in a broad sense).</speaker>
The template is: _Direction → Distance → Dimension_ (area/region).

**FAhA** (direction), **VA** (distance), **VEhA** (extent).

<dl class="drani">
<dt>ca'u</dt><dd>in front of...</dd>
<dt>ri'u</dt><dd>to the right of...</dd>
<dt>zu'a</dt><dd>to the left of...</dd>
<dt>vi</dt><dd>near (the spatial version of **zi**)</dd>
<dt>vu</dt><dd>far (the spatial version of **zu**)</dd>
<dt>ve'i</dt><dd>small region (spatial duration)</dd>
<dt>ve'u</dt><dd>large region (spatial duration)</dd>
</dl>

> le nanmu **ba zi ze'u ca'u vu ve'u** bajra
> _That man will, a bit later, for a long time, run far ahead in a wide area._


<speaker sprite="sor2">That's a lot of little words to stack up, but at least the template is consistent.</speaker>

---

<speaker sprite="sor1">Wait, we didn't use any of these tags in the earlier lessons. What tense were those sentences in?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">_Tense is completely optional._ If you don't use any tags, the time and place are just understood from context—much like how Japanese leave things like singular/plural or tense vague when it's already obvious.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">But will people actually understand me?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Of course! Natural languages leave out massive amounts of detail all the time, and we still communicate just fine.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">Alright, so the lesson is: _omit the tags when it's clear, and add them when you need to be precise._</speaker>
