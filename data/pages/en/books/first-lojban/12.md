## Lesson 12. Tense: time and space

<speaker sprite="koc1">**Tense** is easy: put **PU** cmavo **immediately before the selbri**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>ca</dt><dd>now</dd>
<dt>pu</dt><dd>before (past)</dd>
<dt>ba</dt><dd>after (future)</dd>
</dl>

> mi **ca** citka lo plise
> _I **now** eat an apple._

> mi **pu** citka lo plise
> _I **did / before now** eat an apple._

> mi **ba** citka lo plise
> _I **will / after now** eat an apple._


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>citka</dt><dd>x1 eats x2</dd>
<dt>plise</dt><dd>x1 is an apple …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">Simple. “Tag” means…?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">**ca/pu/ba** are **tags** — more on that later. For now: **tags stick to the selbri.**</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">Not sticky — good.</speaker>
### Compound tags

<speaker sprite="koc2">Add **distance** after **PU**: **zi** (near) / **zu** (far).</speaker>
|   | **zi** | **zu** |
|---|--------|--------|
| pu | a little before | long ago |
| ba | soon | far future |

<dl class="drani">
<dt>zi</dt><dd>short distance in time (after PU)</dd>
<dt>zu</dt><dd>long distance in time (after PU)</dd>
</dl>

> mi **pu zi** citka lo plise
> _I ate an apple a short while ago._

> mi **ba zu** citka lo plise
> _I’ll eat an apple in the distant future._


<speaker sprite="sor2">Direction + distance — neat.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">That’s a **compound tag**. Add **duration**: **ze'i** (short interval), **ze'u** (long interval).</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>ze'i</dt><dd>short extent of interval</dd>
<dt>ze'u</dt><dd>long extent of interval</dd>
</dl>

> mi **pu ze'i** bajra
> _I ran for a short time in the past._

> mi **ba zi ze'u** bajra
> _Soon I’ll run for a long time._


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>bajra</dt><dd>x1 runs on x2 …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor6">Order: **direction — distance — extent**. Okay.</speaker>
### Space: FAhA, VA, VEhA

<speaker sprite="koc1">Same pattern works in **space** — Lojban parallels time and space (**tense** in the broad sense).</speaker>
**Direction — distance — extent:**

**FAhA** (direction), **VA** (distance), **VEhA** (extent).

<dl class="drani">
<dt>ca'u</dt><dd>frontward of …</dd>
<dt>ri'u</dt><dd>to the right of …</dd>
<dt>zu'a</dt><dd>to the left of …</dd>
<dt>vi</dt><dd>near (after FAhA)</dd>
<dt>vu</dt><dd>far (after FAhA)</dd>
<dt>ve'i</dt><dd>small region</dd>
<dt>ve'u</dt><dd>large region</dd>
</dl>

> le nanmu **ba zi ze'u ca'u vu ve'u** bajra
> _That man will, a bit later, for a long time, run far ahead in a wide area._


<speaker sprite="sor2">Many words — but the template is simple.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor1">We didn’t use tense tags earlier — what tense were those?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">**Tense is optional.** Without tags, **when/where is contextual** — like leaving singular/plural vague in Japanese when it still works.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">Will people really follow?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Natural languages omit tons — we still communicate.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So: **omit when clear; add tags when you need precision.**</speaker>
