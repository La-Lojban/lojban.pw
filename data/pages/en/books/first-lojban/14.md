## Lesson 14. Sumtcita (tagged sumti)

<speaker sprite="koc1">We used **tense** and **aspect** tags before the selbri. Tags have **another** use: **before a sumti** — like **prepositions**.</speaker>
A **tagged sumti** can go wherever a sumti can (often sentence-initial or final).

<speaker sprite="sor1">So far tags sat on the selbri — e.g. **le nanmu ba zi ze'u ca'u vu ve'u bajra**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Right. You can also say things like:</speaker>

> **pu lo nu do klama ti kei** ra cliva ti
> _Before you came here, they left here._

> mi ctuca do fo lo lojbo **ca lo bavlamdei**
> _I teach you Lojban tomorrow._

> lo nanla cu cmila **ti'a do**
> _A boy laughs behind you._

> mi tavla lo pendo lo lojbo **ba lo nu penmi do kei** **bu'u la .latcmatcad.**
> _After meeting you, I talk to my friend in Lojban in Latcmatcad._


<speaker sprite="sor2">So tags work adverbially on the selbri **or** preposition-like on sumti.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">**tag zo'e** can shorten to **tag ku** — **ku** pairs with more than just **lo/le**.</speaker>

> **pu ku** mi penmi do
> _I meet you (at some time) before (now)._


<speaker sprite="sor1">Does a tagged sumti modify the whole sentence?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">**Usually think: the tagged sumti scopes over the whole bridi** — e.g. “I meet you; that’s before some reference time.”</speaker>

---

<speaker sprite="koc1">Another tag family: **BAI** — **modal tags** derived from brivla. The tag’s sense comes from the **x1** of the source predicate (and **se/te/ve/xe** shift it like with sumti).</speaker>

> ti grusi jubme **fi'e lo patfu**
> _This grey desk, father created (it)._


<speaker sprite="sor1">So **fi'e** is “by creator x1 of **finti**”.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">**BAI tags inherit x1 of their source** — and **se/te/ve/xe** pull x2–x5, parallel to **lo se klama**.</speaker>
Examples: **bau** (language), **gau** (agent), **ka'a** / **seka'a** (goer / destination), **ka'ai** (together-with), **ki'u** (reason), **pi'o** / **sepi'o** (user / tool).

> mi ciska **bau lo ponjo**
> _I write in Japanese._

> tu cu blanu **gau le nanmu**
> _That is blue by the man’s doing._

> mi lacpu lo bloti **se ka'a lo xamsi**
> _I pull the boat toward the sea._

> la .kocon. cu citka lo grute **ka'ai lo nixli**
> _Koshon eats fruit with the girl._

> mi cortu lo jamfu **ki'u lo nu ze'u bajra kei**
> _My feet hurt because I ran a long time._

> ra ca'o zbasu lo stizu **sepi'o lo xance**
> _They’re making a chair using their hands._
