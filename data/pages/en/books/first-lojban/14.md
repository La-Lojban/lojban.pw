## Lesson 14. Sumtcita (tagged sumti)

<speaker sprite="koc1">We've seen how _tense_ and _aspect_ tags work before the _selbri_. But these tags have _another_ major job: they can be placed _before a_ _sumti_, acting much like _prepositions_ in English.</speaker>
A sumti with a tag in front of it is called a **sumtcita** (tagged sumti). You can place them anywhere a normal sumti can go—most often at the beginning or end of a sentence.

<speaker sprite="sor1">So far tags sat on the selbri — e.g. **le nanmu ba zi ze'u ca'u vu ve'u bajra**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Right. You can also say things like:</speaker>

> **pu lo nu do klama ti kei ra cliva ti**
> _Before you came here, they left here._

> **mi ctuca do fo lo lojbo ca lo bavlamdei**
> _I teach you Lojban tomorrow._

> **lo nanla cu cmila ti'a do**
> _A boy laughs behind you._

> **mi tavla lo pendo lo lojbo ba lo nu penmi do kei bu'u la .latcmatcad.**
> _After meeting you, I talk to my friend in Lojban in Latcmatcad._


<speaker sprite="sor7">I see. So these tags are versatile—they act like adverbs when they're on the selbri, and like prepositions when they're on a sumti.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Exactly. And here's a shorthand: if you want to use a tag with an unspecified place (**zo'e**), you can just use **tag + ku**. It turns out **ku** is a very busy word!</speaker>

> **pu ku** mi penmi do
> _I meet you (at some time) before (now)._


<speaker sprite="sor2">Does the tagged sumti modify the whole sentence, then?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Generally, yes. You can think of the tagged _sumti_ as providing the context for the entire _bridi_. For example: "I meet you; and this whole event took place *before* some reference time."</speaker>

---

<speaker sprite="koc2">We have another family of tags called _BAI_—these are _modal tags_ derived directly from _brivla_. The meaning of the tag corresponds to the **x<sub>1</sub>** slot of its source word. (And just like before, you can use **se**/**te**/**ve**/**xe** to swap those slots!)</speaker>

> **ti grusi jubme fi'e lo patfu**
> _This grey desk, father created (it)._


<speaker sprite="sor7">Ah, so **fi'e** means "by the creator" because it's derived from the x<sub>1</sub> of **finti** (to create).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Precisely! _BAI_ tags _pull out_ the **x<sub>1</sub>** of their source _brivla_. And if you use those swapping words (**se**, **te**, etc.), you can access the other slots, too. It's exactly the same logic we used with **lo se klama**.</speaker>
Here are some common ones: **bau** (in language...), **gau** (done by agent...), **ka'a** (going with...), **ka'ai** (together with...), **ki'u** (for the reason...), **pi'o** (using tool...).

> **mi ciska bau lo ponjo**
> _I write in Japanese._

> **tu cu blanu gau le nanmu**
> _That is blue by the man’s doing._

> **mi lacpu lo bloti se ka'a lo xamsi**
> _I pull the boat toward the sea._

> **la .kocon. cu citka lo grute ka'ai lo nixli**
> _Koshon eats fruit with the girl._

> **mi cortu lo jamfu ki'u lo nu ze'u bajra kei**
> _My feet hurt because I ran a long time._

> **ra ca'o zbasu lo stizu sepi'o lo xance**
> _They’re making a chair using their hands._
