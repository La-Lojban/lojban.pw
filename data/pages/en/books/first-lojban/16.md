## Lesson 16. Negation

<div class="note">

You may notice more vocabulary per chapter from here on: the **skeleton** of Lojban is mostly set, so we widen expression and reading practice.

</div>


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

Simplest negation: put **na** before the **selbri** — “it is not the case that …”

<dl class="drani">
<dt>na</dt>
<dd>negates the bridi; typically immediately before the selbri.</dd>
</dl>


> mi **na** catra le gerku
> _I am not killing the dog._

> la .miran. **na** dasni lo crino kosta
> _Milan is not wearing a green coat._

> mi **na** du la .kocon.
> _I am not Koshon._


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

Like tense tags, **na** before the selbri also marks where the selbri starts — it can stand in for **cu**.

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/sor1.png" caption="Sora" definition=""></pixra>

What about strong affirmation?

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc2.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**ja'a** — affirms the whole bridi (opposite polarity feel to **na**).

<dl class="drani">
<dt>ja'a</dt>
<dd>emphatic affirmation before selbri.</dd>
</dl>


> do **ja'a** ca jmive
> _You **are** alive now._

> ti **ja'a** prenu .i na minji
> _This **is** a person, not a machine._


---

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**NAhE** cmavo tweak the **selbri** itself (parallel slot to **SE**):

<dl class="drani">
<dt>to'e</dt>
<dd>opposite polar meaning</dd>
<dt>no'e</dt>
<dd>neutral midpoint</dd>
<dt>je'a</dt>
<dd>same meaning, emphatic (“really is …”)</dd>
<dt>na'e</dt>
<dd>“other than the usual sense of …” (non-specific contrary)</dd>
</dl>


> mi prami do / mi **to'e prami** do
> _love / “anti-love” (hate-ish)._

> lo vi tanxe cu barda / lo vi tanxe cu **no'e barda**
> _big / neither big nor small._

> lo nu cilre fi lo lojbo gerna cu **je'a zdile** mi
> _Learning Lojban grammar is **really** fun._


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**NAhE** cannot replace **cu** — the **NAhE selbri** chunk may still form **tanru** pieces.

> lo crino **to'e barda**
> _green small-ish thing_

> lo **na'e crino** kosta
> _a non-green coat_


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/sor5.png" caption="Sora" definition=""></pixra>

**na** vs **na'e**?

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**na** negates the **whole claim**. **na'e** builds a **different predicate** (“not green” still implies **coloured** somehow — “suggestive” negation).

> la .miran. **na** dasni lo crino kosta
> _It’s false that Milan wears a green coat (maybe naked, maybe other coat)._

> la .miran. cu dasni lo **na'e crino** kosta
> _Milan wears a **non-green** coat._


<speaker sprite="sor2">**ti na nanmu** vs **ti na'e nanmu** — first: not a man (could be rock); second: suggests **some** gender, not male.</speaker>
<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc5.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

In practice **na** is the safe default; use **NAhE** when you want the narrower “other kind of …” nuance.
