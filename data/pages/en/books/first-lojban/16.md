## Lesson 16. Negation

<div class="note">

You may notice more vocabulary per chapter from here on: the **skeleton** of Lojban is mostly set, so we widen expression and reading practice.

</div>

<speaker sprite="koc1">Up until now, we've mostly dealt with straightforward **statements**. But what if you want to say that something *isn't* the case? It's time to tackle **negation**.

The simplest way is to place the word **na** immediately **before the selbri**. This basically says, "It is not true that..."</speaker>

<dl class="drani">
<dt>na</dt>
<dd>negates the bridi; typically immediately before the selbri.</dd>
</dl>

> mi **na** catra le gerku
> _I am not killing the dog._

> la .miran. **na** dasni lo crino kosta
> _Milan is not wearing a green coat._

> mi **na** du la .kocon.
> _I am not Koshon._

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>catra</dt>
<dd>x1 kills x2 by method x3</dd>
<dt>gerku</dt>
<dd>x1 is a dog of species/breed x2</dd>
<dt>dasni</dt>
<dd>x1 wears x2 as garment x3</dd>
<dt>crino</dt>
<dd>x1 is green</dd>
<dt>kosta</dt>
<dd>x1 is a coat of material x2</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc1">Just like the tense tags we saw earlier, placing **na** before the selbri also clearly marks where the verb begins. It can even take the place of **cu**.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor1">That's remarkably simple! Is there an opposite word? Like a way to say, "Yes, it really *is* true!"</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc2">There certainly is! That would be **ja'a**. It works exactly like **na**, but it **affirms** the entire sentence instead of negating it.</speaker>

<dl class="drani">
<dt>ja'a</dt>
<dd>emphatic affirmation before selbri.</dd>
</dl>

> do **ja'a** ca jmive
> _You **are** (really) alive right now._

> ti **ja'a** prenu .i na minji
> _This **is** a person; it's not a machine._

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>prenu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a person (psychological sense)</dd>
<dt>minji</dt>
<dd>x1 is a machine for purpose x2</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor2">Nice — paired particles that work the same way.</speaker>

---

<speaker sprite="koc1">So, **na** and **ja'a** deal with the truth of the **entire sentence**. But we also have the **NAhE-series** of words, which allow you to tweak the meaning of the **selbri** itself (similar to how **SE** works).

These words let you build **new shades of meaning**: the opposite, a neutral midpoint, or just something "other than" the standard definition.</speaker>

<dl class="drani">
<dt>to'e</dt><dd>the logical **opposite** (polar meaning)</dd>
<dt>no'e</dt><dd>the **neutral midpoint** (not X, not its opposite)</dd>
<dt>je'a</dt><dd>the **strong affirmation** ("it really is [selbri]")</dd>
<dt>na'e</dt><dd>**something else** ("other than the usual sense of [selbri]")</dd>
</dl>

> mi prami do / mi **to'e prami** do
> _I love you / I hate you (the opposite of love)._

> lo vi tanxe cu barda / lo vi tanxe cu **no'e barda**
> _The box is big / The box is medium-sized (not big, not small)._

> lo nu cilre fi lo lojbo gerna cu **je'a zdile** mi
> _Learning Lojban grammar **really is** fun for me._

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>prami</dt>
<dd>x1 loves x2</dd>
<dt>tanxe</dt>
<dd>x1 is a box for contents x2, material x3</dd>
<dt>barda</dt>
<dd>x1 is big in property x2 compared to x3</dd>
<dt>cilre</dt>
<dd>x1 learns fact x2 about subject x3 from source x4 by method x5</dd>
<dt>gerna</dt>
<dd>x1 is the grammar of language x2 regarding property x3</dd>
<dt>zdile</dt>
<dd>x1 is fun for x2 in aspect x3</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc1">Keep in mind that **NAhE** words don't act as sentence markers like **na** or **cu** do. The **[NAhE] + selbri** combination is still treated as a single unit and can still form part of a **tanru**.</speaker>

> lo crino **to'e barda**
> _green small-ish thing_

> lo **na'e crino** kosta
> _a non-green coat (a coat of some other color)_

<speaker sprite="sor5">From the definitions alone, **na** and **na'e** don’t look *that* different…</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc1">**na** negates the **entire claim** (it says the whole sentence is false). **na'e** simply points to a **different predicate** ("not green" still implies we're talking about a color—it's what we call "suggestive negation").</speaker>

> la .miran. **na** dasni lo crino kosta
> _It’s false that Milan wears a green coat (maybe naked, maybe other coat)._

> la .miran. cu dasni lo **na'e crino** kosta
> _Milan is wearing a coat that is **something other than green**._

<speaker sprite="sor2">Oh, I see. So **ti na nanmu** means "This thing is not a man" (it could be a rock for all we know). But **ti na'e nanmu** suggests it's *something like* a man or has a gender, but specifically is *not* a man.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc5">In general, **na** is your safe default for negation. Use the **NAhE** series when you want that specific "it's another kind of..." nuance.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor7">Negate with **na**, refine with **NAhE**. Simple!</speaker>
