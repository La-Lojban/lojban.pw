## Lesson 1. Greetings

**coi rodo** — hello, everyone!

*First Steps in Lojban* (abbreviated **Hajiroji**) has been written since September 2013. As of April 2015 we are moving the site and rolling out **First Steps in Lojban, 2nd edition** (**Hajiroji 2**). It is still unfinished, but we are publishing finished parts as we go.

In the first edition, the text is by **cogas** (“ginger”), and the three cute illustrations were drawn by **Vae**. I am also grateful to **guskant** for continued guidance, to **youxkei** for improving the site source for the migration, and to **PJCG** (Young Lojbanists’ group) and many others — Hajiroji exists thanks to a lot of support. **ki'e sai** — thank you all.

The adaptation of the course to English with new illustrationswas done by **la gleki**.
---

While writing Hajiroji 2, the thing I cared about most was the **“rough-and-ready” level**.

The original goal of Hajiroji was:

**To make an introduction where you think, “Oh, Lojban isn’t such a big deal — it might even be easy!”**

But because of my clumsiness, the first Hajiroji packed in so much that “for beginners” was a stretch. Compared with older introductions like *First Steps in Arca* or *First Steps in Esperanto*, trickier topics had crept in.

So in Hajiroji 2 I tried again to follow those models and aim for a **rough grasp** of Lojban. If you mostly want to **try speaking and writing** without diving into deep theory first, this should fit you.

If you want to savour Lojban in depth, you may find it a bit thin. If you fuss over every detail, it may feel like a course that “doesn’t scratch every itch.”

So Hajiroji 2 adds more **supplementary material** and **FAQ**-style notes for questions that are too fine-grained for the main lessons but come up often.

Another focus is **de facto standard** usage. Lojban’s spec leaves a lot of freedom; explaining all of it at once gets fiddly and heavy.

Here I want **“how most people actually use Lojban”** as the model. Most people stick to ASCII; many write in something like **SVO** order. Enjoying the full freedom of Lojban is fine — but nailing a simple default first is fine too.

---

Even in the Hajiroji 2 era, grammar and semantics keep evolving.

Hajiroji 2 uses **CLL 1.0** as a reference, but for semantics it mostly follows **current BPFK** material. Articles (**le**, **lo**, etc.) in particular may differ from CLL — please keep that in mind.

So: take the telescope called Lojban and have fun looking at the world!
