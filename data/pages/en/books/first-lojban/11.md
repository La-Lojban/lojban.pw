## Lesson 11. Dropping terminators; **cu**

<speaker sprite="sor1">I've been on a bit of a decluttering kick lately.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Oh? Do tell.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">Tossing out old junk just feels *so good*!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">It's a rare sight to see you being so tidy! Want to try some _decluttering in Lojban_ while you're at it?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">What do you mean? Is there junk in the language too?</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc1">The "junk" that most beginners (and even pros) want to get rid of are _terminators_—all those repetitive **ku**'s:

> **lo ninmu ku klama lo tcadu ku lo zdani ku lo zarci ku lo karce ku**
> _The woman goes by car from home via the shop to the city._</speaker>

tcadu
: city …

zdani
: nest/home …

zarci
: shop …

karce
: car …

<speaker sprite="sor4">Ugh, five **ku** tokens in a single sentence! That's a lot of work.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Well, Lojban actually lets you _omit terminators_ whenever your _intended meaning_ is clear to the listener. The golden rule is:

If dropping it doesn't change how the sentence is parsed, you can throw it away.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor2">That sounds like a bit of a judgment call...</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Here are the _ABCs of Elision_:

1. _End-of-sentence terminators_: You can almost always drop these.
2. **ku**: You can usually drop this, _except_ when it's right before the _selbri_.
3. **kei**: Be very careful with this one. _Don't drop it_ unless it's at the end of the sentence.

The key is that your "brackets" must still be unambiguous to the listener.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor1">So:

> **lo ninmu ku klama lo tcadu lo zdani lo zarci lo karce**
</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor2">Wait, why *can't* we drop **ku** right before the selbri?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Look at what happens if you do:

> **lo ninmu klama lo tcadu …**


That turns it into a _tanru_ (_"woman-type goer"_)! The listener won't know where the _description_ ends and the _main verb_ begins.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc4">Precisely.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor4">So that one **ku** has to stay? That's kind of annoying.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">There's a better way! You can use **cu**—the _selbri marker_. It tells the listener "the main verb starts here," and it effectively _closes_ all open _descriptions_ before it.</speaker>
cu
: Marks the start of the selbri; ends all open structure before it.

> **lo ninmu cu klama lo tcadu lo zdani lo zarci lo karce**


<speaker sprite="sor7">Now that's much cleaner!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">And what was the deal with not dropping **kei** in the middle of a sentence?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc3">Words like **nu** and **du'u** are _greedy_—they will keep swallowing the following words into the sub-clause until they hit a **kei**. If you drop **kei**, they might swallow the rest of your sentence! **cu** can also act as a stop-gap, but it's safer to keep the **kei** visible unless it's at the very end.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor7">Got it: **lo**/**le** _descriptions_ are easy to close, so **ku** is often gone. But _abstractions_ (**NU**) are _greedy_, so keep the **kei** around until the very end.</speaker>
