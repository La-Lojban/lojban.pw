## Lesson 11. Dropping terminators; **cu**

<speaker sprite="sor2">I’ve been into decluttering lately.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">…Oh?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">Tossing junk feels *great*!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Rare to see you tidy. Want to **declutter Lojban** too?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">What do you mean?</speaker>

---

<speaker sprite="koc1">The clutter you (and many readers) want gone: **terminators** — all those **ku**:</speaker>

> lo ninmu **ku** klama lo tcadu **ku** lo zdani **ku** lo zarci **ku** lo karce **ku**
> _The woman goes by car from home via the shop to the city._


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>tcadu</dt><dd>city …</dd>
<dt>zdani</dt><dd>nest/home …</dd>
<dt>zarci</dt><dd>shop …</dd>
<dt>karce</dt><dd>car …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor3">Ugh, five **ku** in one bridi!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Lojban lets you **omit terminators** when **what you mean** matches **what the parser gets**. One rule:</speaker>
**Intended meaning = parsed meaning.**

<speaker sprite="sor6">Easier said than judged…</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">**Elision ABC:**</speaker>

1. **End-of-bridi terminators** — almost always droppable.  
2. **ku** except **right before the selbri** — usually droppable.  
3. **kei** — **don’t drop** unless it’s end-of-bridi (be careful).

Equivalently: **brackets must still be unambiguous.**

<speaker sprite="sor1">So:</speaker>

> lo ninmu **ku** klama lo tcadu lo zdani lo zarci lo karce


<speaker sprite="sor1">Why **can’t** we drop **ku** immediately before the selbri?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">If you remove it:</speaker>

> lo **ninmu klama** lo tcadu …


That’s a **tanru** “woman-type goer” — wrong bridi, no clear selbri.

<speaker sprite="koc4">Exactly.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">Only that **ku** must stay — ugly?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Use **cu** — **selbri marker**; it **closes** everything before it like a super-terminator.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>cu</dt>
<dd>Marks the start of the selbri; ends all open structure before it.</dd>
</dl>

> lo ninmu **cu** klama lo tcadu lo zdani lo zarci lo karce


<speaker sprite="sor7">Clean!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Why “don’t drop **kei** mid-stream”?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">**NU** swallows a following bridi; without **kei**, it never stops. **Be careful eliding openers that eat bridi.**</speaker>
**cu** can also stop NU from eating too much — handy.

<speaker sprite="sor2">**lo/le** only eat **selbri**, so **ku** drops often; **NU** is greedy — keep **kei** visible.</speaker>
