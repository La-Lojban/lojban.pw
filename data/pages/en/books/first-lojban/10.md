## Lesson 10. Abstractions (noun clauses)

<speaker sprite="koc1">We’re near the halfway mark — a good time for **“that …”** clauses.</speaker>
From [Lesson 5](/en/books/first-lojban/5), recall:

> **la .kocon. djuno lo du'u la .soran. zvati la .latcmatcad. kei ku**

  — Koshon knows that Sora is in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>djuno</dt>
<dd>x1 knows proposition x2 …</dd>
</dl>

> **mi nelci lo nu cadzu kei ku**
> _I like walking._


> **mi gleki lo nu mi penmi do la .latcmatcad. kei ku**
> _I’m happy I met you in Latcmatcad._


<speaker sprite="sor1">So we have:</speaker>
- **lo nu [bridi] kei ku** — the **event** that [bridi]  
- **lo du'u [bridi] kei ku** — the **proposition** that [bridi]

<speaker sprite="koc1">English has one big “that”; Lojban splits **event** vs **proposition** (and more).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">What’s a “proposition”?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">Loosely: a **truth-apt** content — typical object of believe, doubt, infer, etc.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Fuzzy, but okay.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Dictionaries mark **du'u** vs **nu** on predicates — you’ll pick it up.</speaker>
The pattern:

**lo [NU] [bridi] kei ku**

The **NU** piece is an **abstractor**; people also say **NU cmavo**. Start with **nu** and **du'u**.

<dl class="drani">
<dt>lo nu</dt>
<dd>article + abstractor: event of the following bridi</dd>
<dt>lo du'u</dt>
<dd>article + abstractor: proposition of the following bridi</dd>
<dt>kei</dt>
<dd>terminator closing the abstraction’s bridi</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc1">There are **ka**, **ni**, **si'o**, etc. — skip for now.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor6">Still feels abstract.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Practice:</speaker>
### Exercise

- Translate to English:
  1. **mi badri lo nu le ninmu ku morsi kei ku**  
  2. **lo nu citka lo plise ku kei ku pluka le cipni ku**  
  3. **le nanmu ku birti lo du'u le plise ku kukte kei ku**  
  4. **lo nu tadni la .lojban. kei ku cinri mi**  
  5. **mi jinvi lo du'u do melbi kei ku**

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>badri</dt><dd>x1 is sad about x2</dd>
<dt>morsi</dt><dd>x1 is dead</dd>
<dt>plise</dt><dd>x1 is an apple …</dd>
<dt>pluka</dt><dd>x1 pleases x2 …</dd>
<dt>cipni</dt><dd>x1 is a bird …</dd>
<dt>birti</dt><dd>x1 is certain proposition x2 holds</dd>
<dt>kukte</dt><dd>x1 is tasty to x2</dd>
<dt>tadni</dt><dd>x1 studies x2</dd>
<dt>cinri</dt><dd>x1 interests x2</dd>
<dt>jinvi</dt><dd>x1 opines that x2 …</dd>
<dt>melbi</dt><dd>x1 is beautiful …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor2">Roughly:</speaker>
1. I’m sad that that woman died.  
2. Eating the apple pleases that bird.  
3. That man is certain the apple is tasty.  
4. Studying Lojban interests me.  
5. I think you’re beautiful.

<speaker sprite="koc5">Good — basically **noun clauses** with an explicit closer **kei**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">**kei ku** feels long… So **nu [bridi] kei** is itself a selbri?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Strictly, **abstractors build selbri from bridi** — but you’ll mostly use **lo [NU] … kei ku**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So **nu** ≈ “… is an event”; **du'u** ≈ “… is a proposition.” If you say not to worry, I won’t!</speaker>
