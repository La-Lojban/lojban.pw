## Lesson 15. Copulas: **me** and **du**

<speaker sprite="koc1">A break from tags: **“be verbs”** — how to say **A is B** when B isn’t a plain predicate.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So **A = B** stuff?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Roughly. But **ti mlatu** / **ti bloti** already mean “this is a cat / boat” **without** a copula.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Still need “this **is** Mika” or “that **is** the boat.”</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Right. Lojban extends the idea with **me**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>me</dt>
<dd>Before a sumti: selbri meaning “x1 is among / identical with [that sumti]” (see dictionary for full places).</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc3">**A me B**: either A’s referent is one of B’s referents, all of A’s referents lie inside B’s, or A and B **co-refer**.</speaker>
Think of two collections of “things we’re talking about,” **A** and **B**:

- If every thing **A** picks out is also among the things **B** picks out, you’re in **subset** territory (maybe **A** is one of **B**’s items, or **A**’s group sits entirely inside **B**’s group).
- If **A** and **B** pick out the **same** things, that’s **identity** (co-reference).

So in plain terms: **everything A points at is also pointed at by B** (subset or identity).

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc2.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

> lo vi nanmu cu **me** lo dinju cikre
> _This man is one of the building-repair crew._


<speaker sprite="koc1">**me** is **not** for mechanical **parts** of a whole — use **pagbu** (“part of”).</speaker>
> lo vi klupe cu **pagbu** le minji
> _This screw is part of the machine._


<speaker sprite="sor1">**pagbu** = part relation; **me** = referent overlap / membership.</speaker>
---

<speaker sprite="koc1">For **strict identity** use **du**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>du</dt>
<dd>x1 is identical to x2</dd>
</dl>

> ti **du** la .mikan.
> _This is Mika._

> tu **du** le bloti
> _That is the boat._


**du**, like **me**, is a cmavo that can act as selbri.
