## Lesson 15. Copulas: **me** and **du**

<speaker sprite="koc1">Let's take a break from tags for a bit and talk about **copulas**—the "be-verbs." Specifically, how to say things like **"A is B"** when B isn't just a simple predicate.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">You mean like when we're setting two things equal to each other?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Something like that. Remember, we already have sentences like **ti mlatu** (this is a cat) or **ti bloti** (this is a boat), which don't need any extra words for "is."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">True. But I still don't know how to say something like "This **is** Sora" or "That **is** the specific boat we were talking about."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Good point! Lojban handles this in a few ways. Let's start with **me**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>me</dt>
<dd>Before a sumti: selbri meaning “x<sub>1</sub> is among / identical with [that sumti]” (see dictionary for full places).</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc3">When you say **A me B**, you're saying one of three things: that A is **among** the items in group B, that A is **identical** to B, or that A's group is a **subset** of B's group.</speaker>
Basically, think about "the things A points at" and "the things B points at":

- If every thing A refers to is also something B refers to, you're using **me** (it's either a member of B or a sub-group of B).
- If A and B refer to exactly the same set of things, that's **identity** (co-reference).

In simple terms: **everything that is A is also B.**

<speaker sprite="koc2">For example:</speaker>

> lo vi nanmu cu **me** lo dinju cikre
> _This man is one of the building-repair crew._


<speaker sprite="koc1">Be careful! **me** is **not** used to talk about literal **parts** of a whole (like a wheel being part of a car). For that, we use the word **pagbu**.</speaker>

> **lo vi klupe cu pagbu le minji**
> _This screw is a **part of** the machine._

<speaker sprite="sor1">Got it. **pagbu** for the "part-of" relationship, and **me** for "membership" or overlap.</speaker>

---

<speaker sprite="koc1">If you want to express **strict identity** (A and B are exactly the same thing), we use the word **du**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>du</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is identical to x<sub>2</sub></dd>
</dl>

> ti **du** la .mikan.
> _This is Mika._

> tu **du** le bloti
> _That is the boat._


**du**, like **me**, is a cmavo that can act as selbri.
