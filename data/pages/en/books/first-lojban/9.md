## Lesson 9. Tagging places (FA)

<speaker sprite="sor7">We've covered a lot of ground—simple sentences, _tanru_, articles, _conversion_ words... I think I'm starting to get the hang of this!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Good! Today we're looking at the **FA** _series_. This is mostly a trick for _readability and making sentences more compact_.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">The "FA series"? What's that about?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Example:</speaker>

> **mi klama zo'e zo'e zo'e ti**
> _I go, using this (x<sub>5</sub>)._


<speaker sprite="sor1">Right, we saw that in the [previous lesson](/en/books/first-lojban/#lesson-8-conversion-se).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">It feels a bit clunky with all those **zo'e**'s, doesn't it?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">I guess. But without them, **mi klama ti** would just mean "I go to this place" (x<sub>2</sub>), rather than "using this tool" (x<sub>5</sub>).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">Exactly! _FA_ _words_ allow you to tag a _sumti_ with its _specific place number_, no matter where it appears in the sentence:</speaker>

> **mi klama fu ti**
> _I go... using **this** (x<sub>5</sub>)._

<speaker sprite="sor7">Oh, so we can just skip all the middle "zo'e" slots by tagging the one we actually want!</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>fa</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>1</sub>**</dd>
<dt>fe</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>2</sub>**</dd>
<dt>fi</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>3</sub>**</dd>
<dt>fo</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>4</sub>**</dd>
<dt>fu</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>5</sub>**</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc2">Just one thing to keep in mind: _Conversion_ (_SE_) is always applied _before_ the _FA_ _tags_. So if you use both, the FA tags count according to the *new* converted place structure.</speaker>

### Exercise

- Translate to English:
  1. **mi dunda fi do**
  2. **lo nanmu ku tavla fo la .lojban.**
  3. **lo verba ku ciska fi lo pelxu pelji ku lo pinsi ku**

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>dunda</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> gives x<sub>2</sub> to x<sub>3</sub></dd>
<dt>nanmu</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a man</dd>
<dt>tavla</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> talks to x<sub>2</sub> about x<sub>3</sub> in language x<sub>4</sub></dd>
<dt>verba</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a child …</dd>
<dt>ciska</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> writes text x<sub>2</sub> on medium x<sub>3</sub> with tool x<sub>4</sub></dd>
<dt>pelxu</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is yellow</dd>
<dt>pelji</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is paper …</dd>
<dt>pinsi</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a pencil/crayon</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">(1) I give (something) to you. (2) The man speaks in Lojban. **fo** puts **la .lojban.** into the x<sub>4</sub> slot!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Right! And (3) is the real test. Here's the key rule: after you tag a _sumti_ with a _FA_ word, any following untagged _sumti_ just follow in order from that point. They "fall in line."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Okay, let's see. The _selbri_ is **ciska** (_writes_). The first _sumti_ is "the child". Then we see **fi**, which marks the yellow paper as x<sub>3</sub>. So the next word, **pinsi** (_pencil_), must be... x<sub>4</sub>!

So: The child writes on yellow paper with a pencil.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc5">Spot on! You just combined articles, tanru, and FA tags all in one go.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor3">I'm on fire!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor6">Naturally!</speaker>
