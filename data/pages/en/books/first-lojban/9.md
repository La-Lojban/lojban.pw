## Lesson 9. Tagging places (FA)

<speaker sprite="sor2">We’ve stacked up quite a bit — basic bridi, tanru, articles, SE…</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Time for **FA** — mostly a **readability / compression** trick.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">“FA series”?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Example:</speaker>
> **mi klama zo'e zo'e zo'e ti**
> _I go, using this (x5)._


<speaker sprite="sor1">We’ve seen that.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Lots of **zo'e**, right?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor3">Yeah — but without them **mi klama ti** means “I go to here” (x2), not “using this.”</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Exactly. **FA cmavo** tag the **place number** of the following sumti:</speaker>
> **mi klama fu ti**
> _I go … using **this** (x5)._


<speaker sprite="sor2">So we skip middle zo'e by tagging the late place.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>fa</dt>
<dd>marks next sumti as x1</dd>
<dt>fe</dt>
<dd>… x2</dd>
<dt>fi</dt>
<dd>… x3</dd>
<dt>fo</dt>
<dd>… x4</dd>
<dt>fu</dt>
<dd>… x5</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc1">**Order of operations:** apply **SE** first, **then** **FA** — FA count on the **converted** selbri.</speaker>
### Exercise

- Translate to English:
  1. **mi dunda fi do**  
  2. **lo nanmu ku tavla fo la .lojban.**  
  3. **lo verba ku ciska fi lo pelxu pelji ku lo pinsi ku**

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>dunda</dt>
<dd>x1 gives x2 to x3</dd>
<dt>nanmu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a man</dd>
<dt>tavla</dt>
<dd>x1 talks to x2 about x3 in language x4</dd>
<dt>verba</dt>
<dd>x1 is a child …</dd>
<dt>ciska</dt>
<dd>x1 writes text x2 on medium x3 with tool x4</dd>
<dt>pelxu</dt>
<dd>x1 is yellow</dd>
<dt>pelji</dt>
<dd>x1 is paper …</dd>
<dt>pinsi</dt>
<dd>x1 is a pencil/crayon</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">(1) I give you (something). (2) The man speaks in Lojban. **fo** puts **la .lojban.** in x4.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">(3) is trickier. Rule: **after a FA-tagged sumti, untagged sumti continue in order** — “fall in line.”</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Selbri **ciska**; sumti: child; **fi** marks yellow paper as x3; **pinsi** is next → x4.</speaker>
So: **The child writes on yellow paper with a pencil.**

<speaker sprite="koc5">Perfect — combines articles, tanru, and FA.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">Nailed it.</speaker>
