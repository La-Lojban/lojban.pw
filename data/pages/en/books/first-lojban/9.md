## Lesson 9. Tagging places (FA)

<speaker sprite="sor2">We've covered a lot of ground—simple sentences, **tanru**, articles, _conversion_ words... I think I'm starting to get the hang of this!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Good! Today we're looking at the **FA** _series_. This is mostly a trick for _readability and making sentences more compact_.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">The "FA series"? What's that about?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Example:</speaker>

> **mi klama zo'e zo'e zo'e ti**
> _I go, using this (x<sub>5</sub>)._


<speaker sprite="sor1">Right, we saw that in the [previous lesson](/en/books/first-lojban/#lesson-8-conversion-se).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">It feels a bit clunky with all those **zo'e**'s, doesn't it?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor3">I guess. But without them, **mi klama ti** would just mean "I go to this place" (x<sub>2</sub>), rather than "using this tool" (x<sub>5</sub>).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Exactly! **FA** _words_ allow you to tag a **sumti** with its _specific place number_, no matter where it appears in the sentence:</speaker>

> **mi klama fu ti**
> _I go... using **this** (x<sub>5</sub>)._

<speaker sprite="sor2">Oh, so we can just skip all the middle "zo'e" slots by tagging the one we actually want!</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>fa</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>1</sub>**</dd>
<dt>fe</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>2</sub>**</dd>
<dt>fi</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>3</sub>**</dd>
<dt>fo</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>4</sub>**</dd>
<dt>fu</dt>
<dd>marks the next sumti as **x<sub>5</sub>**</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc1">Just one thing to keep in mind: _Conversion_ (**SE**) is always applied _before_ the **FA** _tags_. So if you use both, the FA tags count according to the *new* converted place structure.</speaker>

### Exercise

- Translate to English:
  1. **mi dunda fi do**
  2. **lo nanmu ku tavla fo la .lojban.**
  3. **lo verba ku ciska fi lo pelxu pelji ku lo pinsi ku**

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>dunda</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> gives x<sub>2</sub> to x<sub>3</sub></dd>
<dt>nanmu</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a man</dd>
<dt>tavla</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> talks to x<sub>2</sub> about x<sub>3</sub> in language x<sub>4</sub></dd>
<dt>verba</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a child …</dd>
<dt>ciska</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> writes text x<sub>2</sub> on medium x<sub>3</sub> with tool x<sub>4</sub></dd>
<dt>pelxu</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is yellow</dd>
<dt>pelji</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is paper …</dd>
<dt>pinsi</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a pencil/crayon</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">(1) I give (something) to you. (2) The man speaks in Lojban. **fo** puts **la .lojban.** into the x<sub>4</sub> slot!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Right! And (3) is the real test. Here's the key rule: _after you tag a **sumti** with a **FA** word, any following untagged **sumti** just follow in order from that point._ They "fall in line."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Okay, let's see. The selbri is **ciska** (writes). The first sumti is "the child". Then we see **fi**, which marks the yellow paper as x<sub>3</sub>. So the next word, **pinsi** (pencil), must be... x<sub>4</sub>!</speaker>
So: **The child writes on yellow paper with a pencil.**

<speaker sprite="koc5">Spot on! You just combined articles, tanru, and FA tags all in one go.</speaker>
