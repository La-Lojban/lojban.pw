## Lesson 5. Getting comfortable with predicates

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/5/latcmatcad-scene.webp" caption="Latcmatcad" definition="Mood illustration for the fictional cat town—no text in the image; name and Lojban form are in the lesson text."></pixra>

<speaker sprite="koc5">Welcome to _Latcmatcad_—the "Cat Town"! In Lojban, we write it as **la .latcmatcad.** (where **la** marks it's a name, and the dots are there to separate it from surrounding words).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">Ooh! Cats! ...Wait, I don't see any...</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc3">That's strange. Maybe they're in a meeting? While we wait for them to show up, let's talk about **.i** and **zo'e**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>.i</dt>
<dd>Sentence boundary / start marker</dd>
<dt>zo'e</dt>
<dd>Unspecified sumti — “something,” “someone,” “who cares for now”</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc1">When you're connecting multiple sentences, you use **.i** to mark the start of each new sentence. It's kind of like a reversed period (Lojban punctuation often feels a bit "backwards" to English speakers!).</speaker>

> **.i ti blabi .i tu xekri**
> _This is white. That is black._


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>blabi</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is white</dd>
<dt>xekri</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is black</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">We didn't use **.i** much in the previous lessons, did we?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">If you're only saying one sentence, you don't strictly need it. But when you're speaking in a flow, it's the standard way to separate your thoughts.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">Got it: use **.i** between sentences. ...And we've seen **zo'e** before too, right?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Exactly. Here's a very handy rule for keeping things concise:

You can almost always drop **zo'e** if it's at the very end of a sentence, or if it's in the x<sub>1</sub> slot (right before the selbri).

In other words: if a slot for "someone/something" is empty, Lojbanists assume you mean **zo'e**.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc5">Oh — cats! Look!</speaker>

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/5/mlatu-single.webp" caption="A cat" definition="Illustration for mlatu in context."></pixra>

<speaker sprite="sor7">Whoa, you’re right — cats!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Say it in Lojban:</speaker>

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>mlatu</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is a cat of species/breed x<sub>2</sub></dd>
</dl>

> **mlatu**
> **zo'e mlatu zo'e**
> _“It’s a cat.” / “There’s a cat.”_


<speaker sprite="koc5">Basically, leading **zo'e** and any **zo'e** hanging off the end of a sentence are optional. So **zo'e mlatu zo'e** can be shortened to just **mlatu**.

Here's the pattern:

- **zo'e** _[selbri]_ _[sumti]_ … **zo'e** **zo'e** … = _[selbri]_ _[sumti]_ …

But be careful! <u>Internal</u> **zo'e** slots that aren't at the end cannot be dropped, or the other _sumti_ will shift into the wrong places:

- _[selbri]_ _[sumti]_ **zo'e** _[sumti]_ ≠ _[selbri]_ _[sumti]_ _[sumti]_</speaker>

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/5/mlatu-many.webp" caption="Many cats" definition="Illustration for so'i mlatu."></pixra>

<speaker sprite="sor5">**mlatu .i mlatu .i mlatu .i mlatu...**</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">Quite the crowd! Let’s take a walk and practice putting sentences together. I’ll give you the words you need, and you just arrange them. Grab a pen and paper—if you're not sure which _sumti_ to use for a slot, just use **zo'e** (and feel free to drop it if it's at the end!).</speaker>

### Composition practice

1) I am in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>zvati</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is at location x<sub>2</sub></dd>
</dl>

- **mi** = _I_
- **la .latcmatcad.** = _Latcmatcad (the so-called place)._

2) Sora goes to Latcmatcad by this car.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>klama</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> goes to x<sub>2</sub> from x<sub>3</sub> via route x<sub>4</sub> by means x<sub>5</sub></dd>
</dl>

- **la .soran.** = _Sora_
- **lo vi karce ku** = _this car_

3) Koshon knows that Sora is in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>djuno</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> knows that proposition x<sub>2</sub> is true, about topic x<sub>3</sub>, in epistemology x<sub>4</sub></dd>
</dl>

- **la .kocon.** = _Koshon_
- **lo du'u la .soran. zvati la .latcmatcad. kei ku** = _the proposition that Sora is in Latcmatcad_

4) I like walking.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>nelci</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> likes x<sub>2</sub></dd>
<dt>cadzu</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> walks on surface x<sub>2</sub> with limbs x<sub>3</sub></dd>
</dl>

- **lo nu cadzu kei ku** = _the event of walking_

5) Many cats are sleeping.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>sipna</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> sleeps</dd>
</dl>

- **lo so'i mlatu ku** = _many cats_

6) I’m happy I met you in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>penmi</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> meets x<sub>2</sub> at x<sub>3</sub></dd>
<dt>gleki</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is happy about x<sub>2</sub></dd>
</dl>

- **do** = _you_
- **lo nu [bridi] kei ku** = _the event that [bridi]_

7) We’re tired, *so* we drink coffee or tea.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>tatpi</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> is tired from x<sub>2</sub></dd>
<dt>pinxe</dt>
<dd>x<sub>1</sub> drinks x<sub>2</sub> from vessel/source x<sub>3</sub></dd>
</dl>

- **mi'o** = _we (you and I)_
- **.i se ki'u bo** = _therefore (replaces the .i boundary)_
- **lo ckafi ku .a lo tcati ku** = _coffee or tea_
