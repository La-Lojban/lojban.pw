## Lesson 5. Getting comfortable with predicates

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/5/latcmatcad-scene.png" caption="Latcmatcad" definition="Mood illustration for the fictional cat town—no text in the image; name and Lojban form are in the lesson text."></pixra>

<speaker sprite="koc2">Welcome to **latcmatcad** — the “cat town”! In Lojban you’ll write the place name as **la .latcmatcad.** (**la** marks a name word; the dots pause so the pieces don’t run together).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">Ooh! Cats! …Wait, there aren’t any…</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc3">Weird — maybe a meeting? While we wait, let’s talk about **.i** and **zo'e**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>.i</dt>
<dd>Sentence boundary / start marker</dd>
<dt>zo'e</dt>
<dd>Unspecified sumti — “something,” “someone,” “who cares for now”</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc1">Between sentences you use **.i** to mark a **new sentence start** — like a reversed period (Lojban often feels “backwards” compared to English punctuation).</speaker>

> .i ti blabi .i tu xekri
> _This is white. That is black._


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>blabi</dt>
<dd>x1 is white</dd>
<dt>xekri</dt>
<dd>x1 is black</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">We haven’t used **.i** much before?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">**Single-sentence** utterances don’t need it at the start. At the beginning of speech it’s optional but fine to add.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So: **put .i between sentences.** …We saw **zo'e** already.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Right. Handy rule:</speaker>

**You may drop zo'e at the end of the bridi, and you may drop zo'e in the x1 slot before the selbri.**

Equivalently: **empty-looking “someone/something” slots are often zo'e.**

<speaker sprite="koc2">Oh — cats! Look!</speaker>

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/5/mlatu-single.png" caption="A cat" definition="Illustration for mlatu in context."></pixra>

<speaker sprite="sor7">Whoa, you’re right — cats!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Say it in Lojban:</speaker>

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>mlatu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a cat of species/breed x2</dd>
</dl>

> **mlatu** = zo'e mlatu zo'e
> _“It’s a cat.” / “There’s a cat.”_


<speaker sprite="koc5">Leading **zo'e** for x1 and trailing **zo'e**s can go — so **zo'e mlatu zo'e** is just **mlatu**.</speaker>
Schematically:

**zo'e [selbri] [sumti] … zo'e zo'e … = [selbri] [sumti] …**

But **internal** zo'e that aren’t at the end **cannot** be dropped:

**[selbri] [sumti] zo'e [sumti] ≠ [selbri] [sumti] [sumti]**

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/5/mlatu-many.png" caption="Many cats" definition="Illustration for so'i mlatu."></pixra>

<speaker sprite="sor5">**mlatu .i mlatu .i mlatu .i mlatu…**</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">Quite a crowd. Let’s stroll and practice **composition**.</speaker> I’ll give you all the words — you just arrange them. Grab pen and paper; when unsure, use **zo'e** (and drop it when it’s a final zo'e).

**1)** I am in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>zvati</dt>
<dd>x1 is at location x2</dd>
</dl>
**mi** = I; **la .latcmatcad.** = Latcmatcad (the so-called place).

**2)** Sora goes to Latcmatcad by this car.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>klama</dt>
<dd>x1 goes to x2 from x3 via route x4 by means x5</dd>
</dl>
**la .soran.** = Sora; **lo vi karce ku** = this car.

**3)** Koshon knows that Sora is in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>djuno</dt>
<dd>x1 knows that proposition x2 is true, about topic x3, in epistemology x4</dd>
</dl>
**la .kocon.** = Koshon; **lo du'u la .soran. zvati la .latcmatcad. kei ku** = the proposition that Sora is in Latcmatcad.

**4)** I like walking.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>nelci</dt>
<dd>x1 likes x2</dd>
<dt>cadzu</dt>
<dd>x1 walks on surface x2 with limbs x3</dd>
</dl>
**lo nu cadzu kei ku** = the event of walking.

**5)** Many cats are sleeping.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>sipna</dt>
<dd>x1 sleeps</dd>
</dl>
**lo so'i mlatu ku** = many cats.

**6)** I’m happy I met you in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>penmi</dt>
<dd>x1 meets x2 at x3</dd>
<dt>gleki</dt>
<dd>x1 is happy about x2</dd>
</dl>
**do** = you; **lo nu [bridi] kei ku** = the event that [bridi].

**7)** We’re tired, **so** we drink coffee or tea.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>tatpi</dt>
<dd>x1 is tired from x2</dd>
<dt>pinxe</dt>
<dd>x1 drinks x2 from vessel/source x3</dd>
</dl>
**mi'o** = we (you and I); **.i se ki'u bo** = therefore (replaces **.i** boundary); **lo ckafi ku .a lo tcati ku** = coffee or tea.

> I am in Latcmatcad. → **[.i] mi zvati la .latcmatcad.**

> Sora goes to Latcmatcad by this car. → **[.i] la .soran. klama la .latcmatcad. zo'e zo'e lo vi karce ku**

> Koshon knows Sora is there. → **[.i] la .kocon. djuno lo du'u la .soran. zvati la .latcmatcad. kei ku [zo'e] [zo'e]**

> I like walking. → **[.i] mi nelci lo nu cadzu kei ku**

> Many cats sleep. → **[.i] lo so'i mlatu ku sipna**

> Happy to meet you there. → **[.i] mi gleki lo nu mi (or [zo'e]) penmi do la .latcmatcad. kei ku**

> We’re tired, so we drink coffee or tea. → **[.i] mi'o tatpi .i se ki'u bo mi'o (or [zo'e]) pinxe lo ckafi ku .a lo tcati ku [zo'e]**

<speaker sprite="koc2">Exactly. Literally “place structure” — the semantic slots of the predicate.</speaker>
