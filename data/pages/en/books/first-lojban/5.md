## Lesson 5. Getting comfortable with predicates

<speaker sprite="koc2">Welcome to **Latcmatcad**—the "Cat Town"! In Lojban, we write it as **la .latcmatcad.** (where **la** marks it's a name, and the dots are there to separate it from surrounding words).</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">Ooh! Cats! ...Wait, I don't see any...</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc3">That's strange. Maybe they're in a meeting? While we wait for them to show up, let's talk about **.i** and **zo'e**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>.i</dt>
<dd>Sentence boundary / start marker</dd>
<dt>zo'e</dt>
<dd>Unspecified sumti — “something,” “someone,” “who cares for now”</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc1">When you're connecting multiple sentences, you use **.i** to mark the start of each **new sentence**. It's kind of like a reversed period (Lojban punctuation often feels a bit "backwards" to English speakers!).</speaker>

> .i ti blabi .i tu xekri
> _This is white. That is black._


<dl class="valsi">
<dt>blabi</dt>
<dd>x1 is white</dd>
<dt>xekri</dt>
<dd>x1 is black</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">We didn't use **.i** much in the previous lessons, did we?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">If you're only saying **one sentence**, you don't strictly need it. But when you're speaking in a flow, it's the standard way to separate your thoughts.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">Got it: **use .i between sentences.** ...And we've seen **zo'e** before too, right?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Exactly. Here's a very handy rule for keeping things concise:

**You can almost always drop a "zo'e" if it's at the very end of a sentence, or if it's in the x1 slot (right before the selbri).**

In other words: if a slot for "someone/something" is empty, Lojbanists assume you mean **zo'e**.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc2">Oh — cats! Look!</speaker>

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/5/mlatu-single.webp" caption="A cat" definition="Illustration for mlatu in context."></pixra>

<speaker sprite="sor7">Whoa, you’re right — cats!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Say it in Lojban:</speaker>

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>mlatu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a cat of species/breed x2</dd>
</dl>

> **mlatu** = zo'e mlatu zo'e
> _“It’s a cat.” / “There’s a cat.”_


<speaker sprite="koc5">Basically, leading **zo'e** and any **zo'e** hanging off the end of a sentence are optional. So **zo'e mlatu zo'e** can be shortened to just **mlatu**.

Here's the pattern:
**zo'e [selbri] [sumti] ... zo'e zo'e ...  =  [selbri] [sumti] ...**

But be careful! **Internal** "zo'e" slots that aren't at the end **cannot** be dropped, or the other sumti will shift into the wrong places:

**[selbri] [sumti] zo'e [sumti]  ≠  [selbri] [sumti] [sumti]**</speaker>

<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/5/mlatu-many.webp" caption="Many cats" definition="Illustration for so'i mlatu."></pixra>

<speaker sprite="sor5">**mlatu .i mlatu .i mlatu .i mlatu...**</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">Quite the crowd! Let’s take a walk and practice **putting sentences together**. I’ll give you the words you need, and you just arrange them. Grab a pen and paper—if you're not sure which sumti to use for a slot, just use **zo'e** (and feel free to drop it if it's at the end!).</speaker>

### Composition practice

**1)** I am in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>zvati</dt>
<dd>x1 is at location x2</dd>
</dl>
**mi** = I; **la .latcmatcad.** = Latcmatcad (the so-called place).

**2)** Sora goes to Latcmatcad by this car.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>klama</dt>
<dd>x1 goes to x2 from x3 via route x4 by means x5</dd>
</dl>
**la .soran.** = Sora; **lo vi karce ku** = this car.

**3)** Koshon knows that Sora is in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>djuno</dt>
<dd>x1 knows that proposition x2 is true, about topic x3, in epistemology x4</dd>
</dl>
**la .kocon.** = Koshon; **lo du'u la .soran. zvati la .latcmatcad. kei ku** = the proposition that Sora is in Latcmatcad.

**4)** I like walking.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>nelci</dt>
<dd>x1 likes x2</dd>
<dt>cadzu</dt>
<dd>x1 walks on surface x2 with limbs x3</dd>
</dl>
**lo nu cadzu kei ku** = the event of walking.

**5)** Many cats are sleeping.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>sipna</dt>
<dd>x1 sleeps</dd>
</dl>
**lo so'i mlatu ku** = many cats.

**6)** I’m happy I met you in Latcmatcad.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>penmi</dt>
<dd>x1 meets x2 at x3</dd>
<dt>gleki</dt>
<dd>x1 is happy about x2</dd>
</dl>
**do** = you; **lo nu [bridi] kei ku** = the event that [bridi].

**7)** We’re tired, **so** we drink coffee or tea.

<dl class="valsi">
<dt>tatpi</dt>
<dd>x1 is tired from x2</dd>
<dt>pinxe</dt>
<dd>x1 drinks x2 from vessel/source x3</dd>
</dl>
**mi'o** = we (you and I); **.i se ki'u bo** = therefore (replaces **.i** boundary); **lo ckafi ku .a lo tcati ku** = coffee or tea.
