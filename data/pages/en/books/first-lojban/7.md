## Lesson 7. Articles

<speaker sprite="sor8">Hrrrgh...</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Good morning! Why the long face?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">I was just going over my notes, and I realized I only know a handful of _sumti_—and most of them are just pronouns like "me" or "you."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">That's a fair point. Today, let’s learn how to turn any word into a _sumti_.</speaker>

### **lo** turns a _selbri_ into a _sumti_

<speaker sprite="sor1">Exactly. How do I talk about a "woman" or a "cat" as a thing? Is there a word for "the" or "a"?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Remember our predicates **ninmu** and **mlatu**?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">"Woman" and "cat". Yep, got it.</speaker>
ninmu
: x<sub>1</sub> is a woman

mlatu
: x<sub>1</sub> is a cat …

<speaker sprite="koc2">Those are _brivla_. If you wrap a _brivla_ (or even a _tanru_) in **lo ... ku**, it turns into a _sumti_ meaning _"the thing(s) that fit the x<sub>1</sub> slot of that predicate."_</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So **lo** is like an article? And what's **ku** for?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">**ku** is a _terminator_—it tells the listener exactly where the _description_ ends. Lojban is very explicit about its structure.</speaker>
lo
: Generic article: “some thing(s) fitting x<sub>1</sub> of the following selbri”

ku
: Terminator closing **lo/le** phrases

<speaker sprite="sor1">So **lo ... ku** acts like brackets around a _description_.

> **lo ninmu ku nelci lo mlatu ku**
> _The woman likes the cat. / Some woman likes some cat._</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc5">Precisely. **lo** _[selbri]_ **ku** basically means "some thing that is (or does) _[selbri]_."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">So with words we know:

- **lo blabi ku** — something white  
- **lo citka ku** — an eater  
- **lo sanli ku** — someone standing  
- **lo cadzu ku** — someone walking  
- **lo klama ku** — someone going  
- **lo sipna ku** — a sleeper  
- **lo gleki ku** — someone happy  
- **lo tatpi ku** — someone tired</speaker>

<speaker sprite="koc2">Just think of **lo** as "pulling out" that first place slot (x<sub>1</sub>). And this works for _tanru_ too:</speaker>

> **lo sipna ninmu ku**
> _sleeping woman_

> **lo stedu xunre finpe citka mlatu ku**
> _red-headed fish-eating cat_


### **la** turns _cmevla_ into _sumti_

<speaker sprite="sor2">**la .soran.**, **la .latcmatcad.** — is **la** also an article?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Yes. **la** before _cmevla_ (name words—the dotted, consonant-final pieces like **.soran.**) means “the thing called …”</speaker>
la
: Name article: “the one(s) called [by the following _cmevla_ / name word(s)]”

> **la .soran.**
> _Sora (so-called)_

> **la .kocon.**
> _Koshon_

> **la .latcmatcad.**
> _Latcmatcad_


<speaker sprite="sor2">No **ku** after **la**?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc3">There are complications…</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor6">Never mind then!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Each _cmevla_ (name word) must _end in a consonant_. If your name ends in a vowel, you just add one—usually **s** or **n**.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor2">So my name becomes **.soran.** Self-intro… **la .soran. cmene mi**?</speaker>
cmene
: Predicate “…is the name of…” (a _brivla_, not a _cmevla_ name token): x<sub>1</sub> (text) is the name of x<sub>2</sub> to namer x<sub>3</sub>

<speaker sprite="koc8"><u>Not quite!</u> **cmene** here is the _predicate_ “x<sub>1</sub> is the name of x<sub>2</sub>”—not the same idea as a _cmevla_ (the name-shaped word **.soran.**). The x<sub>1</sub> of **cmene** has to be the name _text_ (often marked with **zo**), not “whoever **la** points at.” **la .soran.** picks out the person; saying what you’re called uses patterns like **zo .soran. cmene mi**.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So how *should* I introduce myself?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">To say "My name is Sora", you can use **zo** (which we'll cover later) or **me** (which we'll see in [Lesson 15](/en/books/first-lojban/#lesson-15-copulas-me-and-du)):</speaker>

> **zo .soran. cmene mi**
> _“soran” is my name (string)._

> **mi me la .soran.**
> _I am (one of) Sora / I am Sora-ish (we’ll refine **me**)._


Or attitudinal intro in [Lesson 25](/en/books/first-lojban/#lesson-25-attitudinals-ui):

> **mi'e .soran.**
> _I’m Sora._


<speaker sprite="sor7">Simplest is probably **mi'e**.</speaker>

### **le** picks out something _specific_

<speaker sprite="sor2">Wait, doesn't Lojban distinguish between "the cat" and "a cat"?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Not in the way English does. **lo** is our workhorse—it's very general and covers both cases.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">"When in doubt, use **lo**." Got it.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">There's also **le**, which we use for _"the specific one(s) I'm thinking of."_ It's still referring to the x<sub>1</sub> slot, but the speaker is singling it out.</speaker>
le
: Specific article: x<sub>1</sub> of selbri, as singled out by the speaker

<speaker sprite="sor1">Like saying "that one thing I was talking about..."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Roughly. Practically speaking, if you can use **le**, you can almost always use **lo** instead. **le** is just a _more specific tool_ in your kit.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">When in doubt, **lo**—I think I can remember that!</speaker>
