## Lesson 7. Articles

<speaker sprite="koc1">Morning — what’s with the face?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">I was reviewing my notes. I only know a few sumti — mostly pronouns.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Fair point. Today let’s expand **sumti**.</speaker>

### **lo** turns a selbri into a sumti

<speaker sprite="sor1">How do I say “woman” or “cat” as things?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">You remember predicates **ninmu** and **mlatu**?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">**ninmu** and **mlatu** — yep.</speaker>
<dl class="valsi">
<dt>ninmu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a woman</dd>
<dt>mlatu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a cat …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc5">Those are **brivla**. Wrap a brivla (or tanru) with **lo … ku** to mean **“something(s) that fit x1 of that predicate.”**</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">**lo** feels like an article; **ku** is…?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">**ku** is a **terminator** — the closing bracket. Lojban is explicit about structure.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>lo</dt>
<dd>Generic article: “some thing(s) fitting x1 of the following selbri”</dd>
<dt>ku</dt>
<dd>Terminator closing **lo/le** phrases</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">So **lo … ku** is “brackets” around a description.</speaker>

> **lo ninmu ku nelci lo mlatu ku**
> _The woman likes the cat. / Some woman likes some cat._


<speaker sprite="koc2">Nice. **lo [selbri] ku** ≈ “something that is/does [selbri] as x1.”</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So with words we know:</speaker>

- **lo blabi ku** — something white  
- **lo citka ku** — an eater  
- **lo sanli ku** — someone standing  
- **lo cadzu ku** — someone walking  
- **lo klama ku** — someone going  
- **lo sipna ku** — a sleeper  
- **lo gleki ku** — someone happy  
- **lo tatpi ku** — someone tired  

<speaker sprite="koc5">Think: **lo pulls out x1.** Tanru work too:</speaker>

> **lo sipna ninmu ku**
> _sleeping woman_

> **lo stedu xunre finpe citka mlatu ku**
> _red-headed fish-eating cat_


### **la** turns cmene into sumti

<speaker sprite="sor1">**la .soran.**, **la .latcmatcad.** — is **la** also an article?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Yes. **la** before a **cmene** means “the thing called …”</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>la</dt>
<dd>Name article: “the one(s) called [cmene]”</dd>
</dl>

> **la .soran.**
> _Sora (so-called)_

> **la .kocon.**
> _Koshon_

> **la .latcmatcad.**
> _Latcmatcad_


<speaker sprite="sor1">No **ku** after **la**?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc3">There are complications…</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Never mind then!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Outside intro scope for now.</speaker>
**Cmene end in a consonant** — if your name doesn’t, add one (**s** or **n** are common).

<speaker sprite="sor2">So my name becomes **.soran.** Self-intro… **la .soran. cmene mi**?</speaker>
<dl class="valsi">
<dt>cmene</dt>
<dd>x1 (text) is the name of x2 to namer x3</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc3">**Not quite** — **cmene**’s x1 is the **text**, not the person. **la .soran.** is “the one named Sora,” not “the name string.”</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">So what do I say?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">For “my name is …” with **cmene**, use **zo** (later) or **me** (lesson 15):</speaker>

> **zo .soran. cmene mi**
> _“soran” is my name (string)._

> **mi me la .soran.**
> _I am (one of) Sora / I am Sora-ish (we’ll refine **me**)._


Or attitudinal intro in lesson 25:

> **mi'e .soran.**
> _I’m Sora._


<speaker sprite="sor2">Simplest is probably **mi'e**.</speaker>

### **le** picks out something specific

<speaker sprite="sor5">No definite/indefinite articles?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Not officially. **lo** is the workhorse — generic “some x1.”</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">“When in doubt, use **lo**.”</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">There’s also **le**: “the specific one(s) I have in mind” — still x1 of the selbri, but **speaker-identified**.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>le</dt>
<dd>Specific article: x1 of selbri, as singled out by the speaker</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">Like “that thing from yesterday’s show…”</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">Roughly. **If you can say it with le, you can usually say it with lo too** — **le** is a subset of **lo**’s jobs.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">**“When in doubt, lo.”** Got it.</speaker>
