## Lesson 7. Articles

<speaker sprite="sor3">Hrrrgh...</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Good morning! Why the long face?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">I was just going over my notes, and I realized I only know a handful of sumti—and most of them are just pronouns like "me" or "you."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">That's a fair point. Today, let’s learn how to turn any word into a **sumti**.</speaker>

### **lo** turns a selbri into a sumti

<speaker sprite="sor1">Exactly. How do I talk about a "woman" or a "cat" as a thing? Is there a word for "the" or "a"?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Remember our predicates **ninmu** and **mlatu**?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">"Woman" and "cat". Yep, got it.</speaker>
<dl class="valsi">
<dt>ninmu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a woman</dd>
<dt>mlatu</dt>
<dd>x1 is a cat …</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc5">Those are **brivla**. If you wrap a brivla (or even a tanru) in **lo ... ku**, it turns into a sumti meaning **"the thing(s) that fit the x1 slot of that predicate."**</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">So **lo** is like an article? And what's **ku** for?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">**ku** is a **terminator**—it tells the listener exactly where the description ends. Lojban is very explicit about its structure.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>lo</dt>
<dd>Generic article: “some thing(s) fitting x1 of the following selbri”</dd>
<dt>ku</dt>
<dd>Terminator closing **lo/le** phrases</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">So **lo ... ku** acts like brackets around a description.</speaker>

> **lo ninmu ku nelci lo mlatu ku**
> _The woman likes the cat. / Some woman likes some cat._


<speaker sprite="koc2">Precisely. **lo [selbri] ku** basically means "some thing that is (or does) [selbri]."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor2">So with words we know:</speaker>

- **lo blabi ku** — something white  
- **lo citka ku** — an eater  
- **lo sanli ku** — someone standing  
- **lo cadzu ku** — someone walking  
- **lo klama ku** — someone going  
- **lo sipna ku** — a sleeper  
- **lo gleki ku** — someone happy  
- **lo tatpi ku** — someone tired  

<speaker sprite="koc5">Just think of **lo** as "pulling out" that first place slot (x1). And this works for tanru too:</speaker>

> **lo sipna ninmu ku**
> _sleeping woman_

> **lo stedu xunre finpe citka mlatu ku**
> _red-headed fish-eating cat_


### **la** turns cmene into sumti

<speaker sprite="sor1">**la .soran.**, **la .latcmatcad.** — is **la** also an article?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Yes. **la** before a **cmene** means “the thing called …”</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>la</dt>
<dd>Name article: “the one(s) called [cmene]”</dd>
</dl>

> **la .soran.**
> _Sora (so-called)_

> **la .kocon.**
> _Koshon_

> **la .latcmatcad.**
> _Latcmatcad_


<speaker sprite="sor1">No **ku** after **la**?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc3">There are complications…</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor1">Never mind then!</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc2">Names in Lojban (cmene) must **end in a consonant**. If your name ends in a vowel, you just add one—usually **s** or **n**.</speaker>

<speaker sprite="sor2">So my name becomes **.soran.** Self-intro… **la .soran. cmene mi**?</speaker>
<dl class="valsi">
<dt>cmene</dt>
<dd>x1 (text) is the name of x2 to namer x3</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="koc3">**Not quite!** Remember, **cmene**'s x1 slot is for the **name string** (the word itself), not the person who carries that name. **la .soran.** refers to the person named Sora, but the word for "being named" works differently.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor5">So how *should* I introduce myself?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">To say "My name is Sora", you can use **zo** (which we'll cover later) or **me** (which we'll see in Lesson 15):</speaker>

> **zo .soran. cmene mi**
> _“soran” is my name (string)._

> **mi me la .soran.**
> _I am (one of) Sora / I am Sora-ish (we’ll refine **me**)._


Or attitudinal intro in lesson 25:

> **mi'e .soran.**
> _I’m Sora._


<speaker sprite="sor2">Simplest is probably **mi'e**.</speaker>

### **le** picks out something specific

<speaker sprite="sor5">Wait, doesn't Lojban distinguish between "the cat" and "a cat"?</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc1">Not in the way English does. **lo** is our workhorse—it's very general and covers both cases.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">"When in doubt, use **lo**." Got it.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc5">There's also **le**, which we use for **"the specific one(s) I'm thinking of."** It's still referring to the x1 slot, but the speaker is singling it out.</speaker>
<dl class="drani">
<dt>le</dt>
<dd>Specific article: x1 of selbri, as singled out by the speaker</dd>
</dl>

<speaker sprite="sor1">Like saying "that one thing I was talking about..."</speaker>
<speaker sprite="koc4">Roughly. Practically speaking, if you can use **le**, you can almost always use **lo** instead. **le** is just a more specific tool in your kit.</speaker>
<speaker sprite="sor7">**"When in doubt, lo."** I think I can remember that!</speaker>
