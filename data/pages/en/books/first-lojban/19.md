## Lesson 19. Relative clauses and phrases

<div class="box">

This chapter includes some **pedagogical choices** that may differ from other references; for ordinary communication it should be fine.

</div>


<pixra url="/assets/pixra/books/first-lojban/icons/koc1.png" caption="Koshon" definition=""></pixra>

**Relative cmavo** relate **extra material** to a sumti — often a **bridi** (**poi** / **noi**) or another sumti (**pe** / **ne**).

### **poi** / **noi** + **ku'o**

<dl class="drani">
<dt>poi</dt>
<dd>restrictive relative — narrows which referents</dd>
<dt>noi</dt>
<dd>non-restrictive — adds side comment</dd>
<dt>ku'o</dt>
<dd>closes NOI-clause</dd>
<dt>ke'a</dt>
<dd>resumptive pronoun inside the clause (defaults to x1-ish if omitted)</dd>
</dl>


> mi citka ti **poi ke'a lenku**
> _I eat **which of these is cold** (subset)._

> mi citka ti **noi ke'a lenku**
> _I eat **this, which (incidentally) is cold** (comment)._


<speaker sprite="koc4">**poi** **restricts**; **noi** **describes** without changing the base referent set (roughly — Lojban isn’t English commas).</speaker>
### **ku**-internal relatives

<speaker sprite="koc1">**lo selbri poi … (ku)** vs **lo selbri ku poi …** differ in how the restriction composes with **lo** — when in doubt, check examples in reference grammar.</speaker>
### **pe** / **ne** — sumti–sumti links

<dl class="drani">
<dt>pe</dt>
<dd>restrictive “of / related to”</dd>
<dt>ne</dt>
<dd>non-restrictive appositive-style link</dd>
</dl>


**pe sumti** ≈ **poi ke'a srana sumti** (and **ne** ≈ **noi** version).

> lo nicte **pe** lo cabdei
> _tonight of today_

> .e'o ko jgari lo kabri ku **pe** do
> _grab **yours** among the cups._


### **po'u** / **no'u** — identity appositives

**po'u** ≈ **poi ke'a du**; **no'u** ≈ **noi ke'a du**.

> mi **no'u** la .kocon. cu ctuca do
> _I, Koshon, teach you._

> le mikce **po'u** le misno cu zvati lo vi spita
> _The doctor, who is the celebrity, is at the nearby hospital._
