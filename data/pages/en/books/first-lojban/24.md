## Lesson 24. Pronouns and pro-bridi

### Personal and demonstratives

<dl class="drani">
<dt>mi, mi'o, mi'a, ma'a, do, do'o</dt>
<dd>inclusive/exclusive we, you(-all)</dd>
<dt>ti, ta, tu</dt>
<dd>this, that-near-you, that-yonder (<strong>pointing</strong> only)</dd>
</dl>


### Anaphora **ri, ra, ru**

**ri** = repeat **last complete sumti** (not sumti-in-progress; usually not mi/do/… — repeat those directly); **ti/ta/tu/ma/zo'e** may be repeated by **ri** inside an utterance.

> mi cirko lo mapku .i ri crino
> _I lost a hat; **it** is green._


If **ra/ru** are vague, clarify with **ra noi …**.

### Reflexives **vo'a … vo'u**

**vo'a** = main bridi’s x1, **vo'e** = x2, …

> mi cusku lo se du'u **vo'a** prami **vo'i** kei do
> _I say to you that I love you._


### Pro-bridi **go'i** family

**go'i** repeats previous bridi; place **sumti** after **go'i** to **override** slots; **lo go'i** ≈ “the x1 of that bridi”; **lo se go'i** etc. with **SE**.

> .i ti pixra .i **lo se go'i** cu tcati .i **lo te go'e** cu me la .xekir.
> _This is a picture; **its subject** is tea; **its author** is Xekir._


### Assignable **ko'a** / **broda** (with **goi** / **cei**)

**goi ko'a** assigns **ko'a** to previous sumti; **cei broda** assigns **broda** to previous selbri — legalistic / technical prose tools.

### Letter variables

**Initial-letter** sumti for nearby **la / le / lo** phrases are a common shorthand; letters can act as sumti.

### **co'e**

**Vague selbri** — “whatchamacallit predicate,” context-heavy.

> lo moklu be do cu co'e
> _Your mouth is, uh, **you know**._

> .i mi klama lo gusta .i .e'u co'e
> _I’m going to the restaurant — **thoughts?**_
